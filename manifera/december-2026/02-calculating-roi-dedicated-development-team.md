## Content

For Development Managers and CTOs, every hiring decision must be justified to the CFO. When proposing the integration of a dedicated offshore development team, the conversation inevitably turns to one metric: **Return on Investment (ROI).**

Calculating the ROI of software development is notoriously tricky. It is not just about comparing the hourly rate of a local developer versus an offshore developer. True ROI calculations must factor in secondary costs, time-to-market speed, and the opportunity cost of stagnant roadmaps.

Here is how to build a bulletproof financial model for a dedicated development team.

### 1. Calculate the Fully-Loaded Local Cost
The biggest mistake companies make is comparing an offshore invoice to a local employee's base salary. You must calculate the *fully-loaded* cost of a local hire.

In markets like the Netherlands or Singapore, the fully-loaded cost includes:
- Base Salary
- Employer Taxes and Social Security (often 20-30% of base)
- Recruitment Agency Fees (typically 15-25% of first-year salary)
- Hardware and Software Licenses
- Office Space and Utilities
- Paid Time Off, Sick Leave, and Training

A local developer with a base salary of €80,000 often costs the company well over €115,000 annually.

### 2. Compare the Offshore Flat Rate
When you hire a dedicated remote team through a partner like Manifera, you pay a predictable, flat monthly rate. 

This rate is all-inclusive. It covers the developer's salary, taxes, office space in our Vietnam tech hubs, HR management, and high-end hardware. If the developer takes vacation or sick leave, it is managed by the partner, ensuring your budget remains highly predictable.

Typically, this results in a direct financial saving of **40% to 60%** per engineering seat compared to the fully-loaded local cost.

### 3. Factor in the Speed to Market (The Opportunity Cost)
Direct cost savings are only half the equation. The true ROI comes from **velocity**.

If it takes 4 months to recruit, interview, and onboard a local Senior Laravel Developer, your product roadmap stalls for a third of the year. What is the cost of *not* shipping that new feature to your users? How much revenue is lost to competitors?

With an established partner, you can augment your team within 2 to 4 weeks. By accelerating your time-to-market by 3 months, the revenue generated by early feature releases often pays for the offshore team entirely.

### 4. Risk Mitigation
Hiring mistakes are expensive. Terminating an underperforming local employee in Europe involves significant legal hurdles and severance costs. 

With a dedicated offshore team model, flexibility is built into the contract. If a developer isn't the right fit, the partner agency handles the replacement immediately, shielding you from HR liabilities and severance payouts.

### Conclusion
The ROI of a dedicated development team goes far beyond the hourly rate differential. By drastically lowering the fully-loaded cost per seat, accelerating time-to-market, and mitigating HR risks, offshore partnerships provide a massive financial advantage.

## FAQ

### How do we measure the productivity of an offshore team?
Productivity should be measured exactly as it is for your in-house team: through Agile metrics. Use sprint velocity, burndown charts, code review turnaround times, and the successful delivery of user stories to track the output of your dedicated remote developers.

### What happens if an offshore developer leaves the company?
High attrition is a risk in any market. However, when you work with a partner like Manifera, the risk is transferred to us. We handle the knowledge transfer and provide a qualified replacement from our talent pool rapidly, ensuring your project experiences minimal disruption.

### Is the offshore flat rate truly all-inclusive?
Yes. A reputable offshore software development company will provide a transparent monthly rate that includes the developer's salary, office infrastructure, hardware, HR management, and local taxes. There are no hidden fees.

### How do I justify an offshore team to my internal engineers?
Frame it as augmentation, not replacement. Offshore teams are brought in to handle the backlog, allowing your senior in-house engineers to focus on high-level architecture and complex problem-solving. It alleviates burnout and helps the entire department succeed.
