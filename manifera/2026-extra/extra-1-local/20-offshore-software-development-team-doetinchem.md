"Just hire two more developers." It's the sentence a CFO hears in every roadmap planning meeting, and the one that never accounts for the four months of recruiting in a tight regional labor market, the €8,000-plus agency fee per hire, or the very real chance the candidate accepts a counteroffer the week before their start date.

**The Pain:** A CFO at a manufacturing-adjacent company based in Doetinchem is weighing two paths to close an engineering capacity gap: keep trying to hire locally in a labor market where mid-sized Achterhoek-region towns compete against Arnhem, Nijmegen, and remote-friendly employers for the same small pool of senior developers, or build an offshore software development team instead.

**The Agitation:** Every month spent unsuccessfully recruiting locally is a month the product roadmap doesn't move, while competitors with functioning engineering capacity keep shipping. Regional manufacturing and logistics firms outside the Randstad routinely report six-plus month vacancies for senior developer roles, and the fully loaded cost of a failed or prolonged local hiring search — recruiter fees, hiring manager hours, delayed projects — frequently exceeds what a properly structured offshore team would have cost for the same period.

## The Architectural Mandate

The decision a CFO actually faces isn't "offshore versus local" in the abstract — it's a choice between three concrete team structures, each with a different cost and risk profile. Building an in-house team from a small regional labor pool means competing on salary against companies with deeper pockets or more prestigious brand names, plus absorbing the full cost of recruiting, benefits, and the six-to-twelve month ramp before a new hire is fully productive. Staff augmentation through an offshore vendor lowers the salary line but keeps all the management overhead in-house, requiring an internal technical lead with the bandwidth to direct day-to-day work. A dedicated offshore pod — a fixed team managed by the vendor's own technical leadership, reporting into your roadmap rather than needing to be micromanaged — is usually the structure with the lowest total cost and the fastest time to productive output, because it doesn't ask your organization to build management capacity it doesn't already have.

Onboarding velocity is where the financial case is won or lost. A properly structured offshore team with standardized processes should reach full productivity within three to four weeks. A team without that discipline, or an in-house hire ramping into an unfamiliar codebase, routinely takes two to three months — a gap that compounds directly into missed roadmap milestones.

IP protection deserves the same rigor a CFO would apply to any vendor contract involving proprietary processes — source code ownership, signed IP assignment, and confidentiality terms specific to any manufacturing or operational data the software touches, which matters particularly for companies in Doetinchem's Achterhoek region where family-owned industrial and manufacturing firms often run decades of accumulated process knowledge through custom internal software.

Communication cadence closes the loop: a dedicated pod with a named point of contact and a predictable weekly reporting rhythm gives a CFO the same visibility into engineering spend and output that they'd expect from any other cost center — something ad hoc staff augmentation rarely provides without significant internal effort.

## The Economics

A six-month unfilled senior developer requisition costs far more than the salary never paid. Between recruiter fees (often 20-25% of first-year salary), hiring manager hours spent screening and interviewing, and the delayed roadmap value of whatever that developer would have shipped, a prolonged vacancy routinely costs a company €40,000-€60,000 in fully loaded opportunity cost before counting the compounding effect of a delayed project on other downstream initiatives. A dedicated offshore pod, properly structured, typically costs less than a single senior local hire's fully loaded salary while delivering the output of a full team.
