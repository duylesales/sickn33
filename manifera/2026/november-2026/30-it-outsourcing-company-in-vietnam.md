## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### (Scenario: CTO analyzing vendor churn) Why is high developer attrition so financially destructive to a project?
Every time a developer leaves, they take critical, undocumented domain knowledge (Tribal Knowledge) with them. It takes a new developer 4 to 8 weeks of paid time just to read the codebase and understand the architecture before they can write a useful feature. High churn means you are constantly paying for 'learning time' instead of 'coding time,' destroying your OpEx ROI.

### (Scenario: CEO evaluating global risk) Why is Vietnam emerging as the superior alternative to traditional tech hubs?
Vietnam has a massive, young population heavily incentivized by government STEM initiatives. Unlike legacy hubs that often focus on low-skill BPO (call centers/data entry), Vietnam's tech culture is intensely focused on high-end software engineering, algorithms, and AI. This results in a workforce that values deep technical mastery and long-term project stability over constant job-hopping.

### (Scenario: VP of Engineering worried about communication) Does the timezone difference with Vietnam cause project delays?
Not with the Hybrid Hub model. In a standard offshore setup, if you ask a question at 3 PM, you wait until the next day for an answer. With Manifera, our Amsterdam-based Technical Architects and Product Owners are in the European timezone. They manage the immediate strategic communication, blocking issues instantly and ensuring the Vietnamese Pods have perfectly clear requirements to execute asynchronously.

### (Scenario: Lead Architect ensuring quality) Do Vietnamese developers integrate well with modern CI/CD and Agile practices?
Exceedingly well, provided there is strict governance. While raw talent is abundant, our Amsterdam hub provides the rigorous framework. We train and mandate our Vietnamese Pods to operate using elite Western standards: strict Trunk-Based Development, automated AST Linting, and aggressive Unit Testing (TDD), ensuring the code quality matches or exceeds internal teams.

### (Scenario: CPO tired of 'Yes-Men') We need an offshore team that pushes back on bad ideas, not just takes orders. Is that possible?
This is the fatal flaw of traditional 'body shops'—they blindly build bad features to bill more hours. Our Amsterdam Product Owners act as your strategic filter. We interrogate your backlog, demand ROI justification for features, and push back on scope creep. We then deliver a lean, mathematically verified architectural blueprint for the Vietnamese Pod to execute flawlessly.
