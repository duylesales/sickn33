🚩 **When offshore IT projects fail, it’s rarely because the developers forgot how to code. It fails because the contract was a trap.**

Signing an MSA with an **IT software development company** is a moment of optimism. But optimism doesn't write code. 

CEOs and Founders, before you sign, audit the vendor contract for these 4 massive red flags:

1️⃣ **The "Proprietary Core" Trap (Vendor Lock-in):** They quote a cheap price, but the contract says the app is built on their "proprietary internal framework." If you fire them, you legally can't take the code with you. 
2️⃣ **Hostage-Style AWS Access:** If the agency creates the AWS/Cloud account under their name, they have the power to shut down your live production servers in a dispute. *You must own the Root Access.*
3️⃣ **The Bait-and-Switch Seniority:** They pitch you a brilliant Senior Architect to win the deal, then quietly swap them for junior bootcamp grads on Day 1. Demand a "Key Personnel" clause.
4️⃣ **Change Request Warfare:** In fixed-price contracts, vendors will claim that a basic UI bug fix isn't in the PDF, charging you €150/hour to fix their own mistakes.
