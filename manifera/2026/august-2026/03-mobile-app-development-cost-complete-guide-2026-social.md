💰 **"How much does an app cost?" → Google says €5K - €500K. That's useless.**

Here's what companies actually pay in Q2 2026, broken into 4 tiers:

📱 **Tier 1 — Simple App (€15K-€40K)**
5-10 screens, Firebase backend, single user role. Think: company directory, event app.
Timeline: 6-10 weeks.

📱 **Tier 2 — Moderate App (€40K-€120K)**
10-25 screens, custom backend, payments, push notifications, admin panel.
Think: marketplace MVP, fitness app.
Timeline: 12-20 weeks.

📱 **Tier 3 — Complex App (€120K-€300K)**
Real-time features, compliance (KYC/AML), offline mode, 25-50+ screens.
Think: fintech app, healthcare platform.
Timeline: 20-40 weeks.

📱 **Tier 4 — Enterprise Platform (€300K-€1M+)**
Multi-app ecosystem, microservices, AI/ML, SOC2 compliance.
Think: super app, banking platform.

⚠️ The costs nobody tells you about:
→ App Store rejections (Apple rejects 36% of first submissions)
→ Maintenance: 20-25% of build cost EVERY YEAR
→ Security audits: €10K-€30K annually
→ Cloud infra: €500-€5K/month

💡 The 2026 platform cheat sheet:
React Native = 90% of projects. Period.
Going dual native (Swift + Kotlin) costs 1.8-2.2x more.
Only justified for AR/VR, Bluetooth IoT, or gaming.

As Marty Cagan said: *"The question is never 'How much does an app cost?' The question is 'How much does the wrong app cost?'"*
