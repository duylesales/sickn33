> *"The most expensive software is the software that almost does what you need."* — **Martin Fowler**, Chief Scientist at ThoughtWorks

Every quarter, a CTO somewhere approves a €200,000 Salesforce implementation convinced it will solve every problem. Eighteen months later, they have spent €450,000 on customizations, hired two full-time Salesforce admins, and the sales team still keeps a shadow spreadsheet because the workflow does not match how they actually sell.

The "buy vs. build" decision is the single most consequential technology choice a growing company makes. And in 2026, the economics have shifted dramatically — in ways most analyses published before the AI-assisted development era simply do not account for.

## The Off-The-Shelf Illusion: Why Licensing Costs Are the Smallest Line Item

When a CFO evaluates an off-the-shelf SaaS product, they see a predictable monthly subscription. What they do not see is the iceberg beneath it.

**The hidden cost structure of "buying" software:**

|---|---|---|---|---|
| License/subscription | €24,000 | €26,400 | €29,040 | €139,128 |
| Implementation partner | €80,000 | — | — | €80,000 |

| Training & change management | €15,000 | €5,000 | €5,000 | €35,000 |
| Admin headcount (1 FTE) | €65,000 | €67,000 | €69,000 | €339,000 |
| Vendor price increases (avg 10%/yr) | — | included | included | included |
| **Total** | **€219,000** | **€113,400** | **€115,040** | **€682,128** |

> *"People routinely underestimate the cost of integrating purchased software into their existing ecosystem by a factor of 3x to 5x."* — **Grady Booch**, IBM Fellow and co-creator of UML

The moment you need a SaaS product to behave differently from its default workflow, you are no longer "buying" software. You are paying a premium to fight against someone else's architectural decisions.

## When Custom Software Development Actually Wins

[Custom software development](https://www.manifera.com/services/custom-software-development/) is not always the right answer. But it is definitively the right answer in three specific scenarios:

### Scenario 1: Your Business Process IS Your Competitive Advantage

If the way you operate is what differentiates you from competitors, forcing that process into a generic tool is strategic suicide. A logistics company whose routing algorithm is 15% more efficient than the industry standard cannot afford to use the same TMS as every competitor.

> *"Software is eating the world, but custom software is what lets you eat your competitors."* — **Marc Andreessen**, General Partner at Andreessen Horowitz (a16z)

### Scenario 2: Integration Complexity Exceeds 3+ Systems

When your new tool needs to exchange real-time data with more than three existing systems (ERP, CRM, data warehouse, payment processor), the integration cost alone will exceed the cost of building a purpose-built solution. Every API connector you build against a SaaS product's API is a dependency you do not control. When they deprecate that API version — and they will — you are rebuilding on their timeline.

### Scenario 3: You Need It For 5+ Years

The breakeven point for custom vs. off-the-shelf typically hits between Year 3 and Year 4. After that, custom software becomes dramatically cheaper per year because you are not paying recurring license fees or vendor markup on every seat.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Design & architecture | €40,000 | — | — | €40,000 |
| Development (offshore team) | €120,000 | — | — | €120,000 |
| QA & testing | €25,000 | — | — | €25,000 |

**Net 5-year savings: €303,477** — with full IP ownership and zero vendor lock-in.

## FAQ

### What is the typical breakeven point between custom software and SaaS licensing? (Scenario: Mid-Market Scale-Up Evaluation)
For a mid-market company with 50-200 users, the breakeven point typically falls between Month 30 and Month 42. Before that point, the upfront investment in custom development exceeds cumulative SaaS licensing costs. After that point, the absence of per-seat licensing fees and vendor price increases makes custom software progressively cheaper each year. The exact crossover depends heavily on the number of integrations required and the complexity of customizations needed in the SaaS alternative.

### How does AI-assisted development impact the cost of building custom software in 2026? (Scenario: CTO Budget Planning)
AI coding assistants like GitHub Copilot and Cursor have reduced the development time for standard CRUD operations, API endpoints, and test scaffolding by approximately 30-40%. For a typical €150,000 custom project, this translates to roughly €45,000-€60,000 in savings on development hours. However, these tools do not reduce the cost of architectural design, system integration logic, or security hardening — which is why experienced senior engineers remain non-negotiable. The net effect is that custom development has become 25-30% more cost-effective compared to 2023 benchmarks.

### What happens to my custom software if the development partner goes out of business? (Scenario: Risk-Averse Enterprise CTO)
This is the single most valid concern about custom development. The mitigation is contractual and architectural. First, your contract must guarantee 100% IP ownership and full source code access from Day 1 — not upon project completion. Second, the codebase must use mainstream, well-documented frameworks (React, Node.js, Laravel, .NET) so any competent development team can maintain it. Third, demand CI/CD pipeline documentation and infrastructure-as-code so your hosting is reproducible. At Manifera, we enforce all three by default because we have seen what happens when vendors do not.

### Should a startup with limited funding choose custom development or SaaS tools? (Scenario: Pre-Seed Founder With €500K Runway)
Almost always start with SaaS. At the pre-seed and seed stage, your business model is still a hypothesis. Building custom software for a workflow you will change six times in the next 18 months is burning cash on concrete when you need to be iterating on paper. Use Notion, Airtable, Zapier, and off-the-shelf tools to validate your processes. Once you have proven product-market fit and your operational workflows have stabilized — typically after Series A — that is when custom software development becomes the smart investment.

### How does Manifera ensure quality when the development team is offshore? (Scenario: European Enterprise Compliance)
Three structural controls. First, every project has a Technical Lead based in our Amsterdam office who owns architecture decisions, conducts code reviews, and joins every client sprint review. Second, our Vietnam and Singapore teams follow ISO 27001-aligned development practices with mandatory automated testing coverage above 80%. Third, we use trunk-based development with CI/CD pipelines that block any merge failing automated security scans (SonarQube, Snyk). The client has real-time access to the repository, the Jira board, and the CI/CD dashboard — full transparency, not quarterly reports.
