🏗 **The most expensive mistake a CTO can make? Letting junior developers choose your software stack.**

Developers inherently suffer from "Shiny Object Syndrome." If you let them, they will build your mission-critical enterprise app using a bleeding-edge framework that was released three weeks ago. 

Two years later? The framework is abandoned. You can't find developers to hire. Security patches stop. You are forced to spend €300,000 on a complete rewrite.

**Stop practicing Hype-Driven Development (HDD).** When choosing a 2026 **software stack**, your goal is survival, not GitHub stars.

Here is the CTO guide to choosing "Boring" tech:
1️⃣ **Frontend:** Stick to **React.js**. It has the largest global talent pool and the best AI-Copilot integration. Don't risk your UI on niche, experimental frameworks.
2️⃣ **Backend:** Boring is beautiful. Use **.NET (C#)** for indestructible enterprise apps, or **Node.js/Laravel** for high-velocity SaaS. 
3️⃣ **Database:** Unless you are logging massive IoT sensor data, use **PostgreSQL**. Relational data integrity is non-negotiable.
4️⃣ **Infrastructure:** Don't lock yourself into proprietary AWS Serverless functions. Use **Docker & Kubernetes**. Containerize your app so you can migrate to Azure or a private EU Cloud whenever you want.
