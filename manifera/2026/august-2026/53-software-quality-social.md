📉 **Software quality is not an engineering metric. It is a financial metric.**

If your CEO tells the CTO to "just ship it and we'll fix the bugs later," they are creating Technical Debt. 

Technical debt is exactly like financial debt. Taking a shortcut allows you to move fast today (like getting a mortgage). But if you don't pay down the principal, the "interest" payments will consume your entire cash flow.

How do you pay the interest? 
❌ **Developer Time:** It takes 8 days to build a feature that should take 2 days, because they have to untangle spaghetti code first.
❌ **The Margin Death Spiral:** Velocity slows down. Management hires more developers to fix the speed. The new developers introduce more bugs. Payroll doubles, output halves, SaaS margins die.

*"A little bit of technical debt helps you move fast. Too much bankrupts you."*
