🔥 **"Buy vs. Build" is a €300K question. Most CTOs get the math wrong.**

Every quarter, a CTO approves a €200K Salesforce implementation. 18 months later: €450K spent, 2 full-time admins hired, and the sales team still uses a shadow spreadsheet.

Here's what the real 5-year TCO comparison looks like:

📊 **Off-the-shelf SaaS (5-year total): ~€682K**
- License fees that compound 10%/year
- €80K implementation partner
- €89K in custom integrations (because it never works out-of-the-box)
- A full-time admin you didn't budget for

📊 **Custom software (5-year total): ~€379K**
- Higher Year 1 cost → dramatically lower Years 2-5
- Zero per-seat licensing
- Full IP ownership
- No vendor lock-in

💡 The breakeven? Month 30-42 for most mid-market companies.

The 2026 twist: AI-assisted coding tools cut boilerplate development by 30-40%. Custom development is cheaper today than at any point in the last 20 years.

⚠️ But here's the honest truth: if you're pre-seed with €500K runway, DON'T build custom. Use SaaS to validate. Build custom after product-market fit.

As Martin Fowler put it: *"The most expensive software is the software that almost does what you need."*
