💸 **The CEO wants to cut the IT budget. So they ask: "Can we hire cheaper coders?"**

This is a fundamental misunderstanding of the **stages software development**.

In enterprise software, typing syntax accounts for less than 20% of the 3-year Total Cost of Ownership (TCO).

If you slash the coding budget by 50% using the cheapest offshore freelancers, you save 10% of your total budget. But you explode the costs in the other 80% of the lifecycle.

Here is where the budget actually burns:
🔥 **Phase 1 (Discovery):** Skip it, and a €500 whiteboard fix becomes a €25,000 database migration in Month 6.
🔥 **Phase 2 (Design):** Don't prototype in Figma. Let developers design in code. Watch your budget vaporize at €80/hour.
🔥 **Phase 4 (QA):** Skip automated tests. Rely on manual clicking. Watch your deployment cycle grind to a halt.
🔥 **Phase 5 (Maintenance):** The eternal burn. A codebase built without architecture costs 3x more to update and eventually requires a total rewrite.

As the old axiom goes: *"A week of coding can save you an hour of thinking."*
