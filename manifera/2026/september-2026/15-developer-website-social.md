💻 **Judging a software agency by their marketing website is a fatal procurement error.**

An IT Director hires an offshore agency because their **developer website** has stunning 3D WebGL animations. Six months later, the agency delivers a logistics platform. The UI is beautiful, but the backend is a disaster. The database locks up under 50 users and the API lacks basic authentication. 

Why? Because building a beautiful frontend marketing site requires a completely different cognitive skill set than architecting highly concurrent backend Systems Engineering.

*"Evaluating backend engineers based on their marketing website is like evaluating a heart surgeon based on their business card's font."*

Elite CTOs don't look at an agency's website. They audit the engineering exhaust:
✅ **Git Commit History:** Are commits massive and chaotic ("Fixed stuff"), or small and semantic?
✅ **Pull Request Reviews:** Is there rigorous architectural debate from a Tech Lead, or just blind merging?
✅ **Infrastructure as Code:** Do they manually click buttons in AWS, or do they use Terraform?
