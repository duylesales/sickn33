📈 **At 3 engineers, everything works. At 30, everything breaks — unless you design for it.**

Here's what we learned scaling a European SaaS team from 3 to 30 through a Dutch-Vietnamese model:

**Phase 1: 3 engineers**
→ CTO manages directly. Fast. Informal.
→ BUT: CTO spends 60% on task management, zero docs, single points of failure
→ Lesson: Start documenting NOW. ADRs, coding standards, onboarding guides.

**Phase 2: 10 engineers (2 squads)**
→ Added a Vietnamese Tech Lead (handles 90% of daily decisions)
→ Added a Dutch PM in Amsterdam (client communication bridge)
→ BUT: Cross-squad dependencies cause sprint blocking

**Phase 3: 30 engineers (5 squads)**
→ 5 squads × 5-6 devs, organized by business capability
→ New roles: Engineering Manager, Principal Architect, Product Owners
→ RFC process for cross-squad changes
→ Weekly architecture guild

**The results:**
→ 4.2× velocity increase (not 10× — and that's realistic)
→ 12 prod deployments/week
→ Bug escape rate: 2.1 per 1,000 (industry avg: 5-8)

**4 counterintuitive lessons:**
1. Scaling is NOT linear. 30% overhead for coordination.
2. Add process RELUCTANTLY.
3. First non-coding hire > 3 additional developers.
4. Culture must be intentionally designed at scale.
