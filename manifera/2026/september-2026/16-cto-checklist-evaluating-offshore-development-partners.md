After evaluating dozens of offshore partners over 11 years, our team distilled the assessment into a structured 20-point checklist. Score each item from 0-3. Any partner scoring below 45/60 is a risk. Below 35/60 is a guaranteed disaster.

## Technical Capability (6 items, max 18 pts)

- [ ] **Tech stack alignment** — Do they have demonstrable experience in your specific technology stack? Can they show production projects?
- [ ] **Architecture competence** — Can a senior architect produce a high-level design for your project during evaluation?
- [ ] **Code quality evidence** — Can they share sample code or conduct a live coding session during evaluation?
- [ ] **DevOps maturity** — Do they have CI/CD pipelines, containerization, and infrastructure-as-code practices?
- [ ] **Testing culture** — What is their minimum test coverage standard? Do they have dedicated QA engineers?
- [ ] **Security practices** — Do they implement SAST/DAST scanning, dependency vulnerability checks, and secure coding guidelines?

## Process Maturity (5 items, max 15 pts)

- [ ] **Agile methodology** — Can they describe their sprint workflow, including planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives?
- [ ] **Documentation standards** — Do they produce architecture decision records, API documentation, and onboarding guides?
- [ ] **Change management** — Is there a formal process for scope changes with impact assessment before implementation?
- [ ] **Estimation accuracy** — Can they share historical data on estimated vs. actual project timelines?
- [ ] **Incident response** — Do they have a defined process for production issues, including escalation paths and SLAs?

## People & Culture (4 items, max 12 pts)

- [ ] **Team transparency** — Can you interview the actual engineers who will work on your project?
- [ ] **Retention rates** — What is their average developer tenure? Below 18 months is a warning sign.
- [ ] **Communication proficiency** — Can developers communicate effectively in English during technical discussions?
- [ ] **Cultural bridge** — Do they have a client-facing project manager who understands your business culture?

## Contractual & Legal (5 items, max 15 pts)

- [ ] **IP ownership** — Does the contract explicitly transfer all IP to you upon delivery?
- [ ] **Governing law** — Is the contract governed by a strong IP jurisdiction (Dutch, UK, Singapore law)?
- [ ] **Data protection** — Is there a GDPR-compliant data processing agreement?
- [ ] **Exit clause** — What happens if the engagement fails? Is there a code handover process?
- [ ] **Replacement guarantee** — What is the SLA for replacing a team member who leaves? (Target: 2 weeks)

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Schedule 45-minute evaluation calls with 3-5 shortlisted partners
2. Score each item honestly during the conversation
3. Request portfolio references for any item you cannot assess directly
4. Eliminate any partner scoring below 45/60
5. Conduct a paid pilot project (2-4 weeks) with the top 2 candidates before committing

## FAQ
### Should I evaluate only offshore partners, or also compare against local options?
Always compare. Include at least one local option in your evaluation to calibrate costs and capabilities. The checklist works equally well for local and offshore partners — but you will likely find that the scores are similar while the costs differ significantly.

### How many partners should I evaluate?
Three to five. Fewer than three does not give you enough comparison data. More than five creates evaluation fatigue and delays your project timeline.

### What if a partner scores high but has no experience in my specific industry?
Industry-specific experience is less important than technical competence and process maturity. A strong engineering team can learn your domain in 2-4 weeks. A weak team with industry experience will still produce bad code.
