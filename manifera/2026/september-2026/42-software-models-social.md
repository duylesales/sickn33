🏗️ **Microservices are an organizational solution, not a technical one. Do not build them prematurely.**

An ambitious new CTO decides their startup's Monolithic **software models** are outdated. They freeze product development for 9 months to rewrite the app into 15 Microservices, "just like Netflix." 

When it launches, engineering velocity drops to zero. Adding a simple feature requires coordinating 4 repositories. Tracking a bug is impossible. And the AWS bill quadruples due to the "Distributed Systems Tax" (network latency and Kubernetes overhead). 

If you have a 30-person engineering team, you do not need Microservices. You are trading simple code complexity for catastrophic operational complexity. 

Elite engineering teams (like Shopify) embrace the **Majestic Monolith**. A single deployable unit with strict, clean internal module boundaries. It delivers blistering development speed with zero DevOps overhead.
