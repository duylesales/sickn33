🏢 **You cannot ship a great product from a broken org chart.**

A massive logistics enterprise decides it is now **a software company**. The CEO tells the siloed IT Department to build a SaaS platform. 
The IT department has a "Database Team," a "Frontend Team," and an "Ops Team."

18 months later, the software launches. It is a disaster. Users have to log into three different portals. The data is disconnected. It feels like 5 different companies built it. 

The CEO collided with Conway's Law: "Software architecture will perfectly mirror the organization's human communication structure."

If your teams require formal tickets to talk to each other, your software will be fragmented and rigid. 
True software companies use Cross-Functional Pods (Frontend, Backend, QA, PM all on one unified team organized around a business goal).
