✨ **If your engineers are excited by your Tech Stack, you have chosen the wrong Tech Stack.**

A startup lets its junior developers pick the **programmers tools** for the new MVP. The developers choose an experimental graph database and a brand-new obscure functional programming language because it looks "cool."

Three months later, the experimental database suffers a memory leak. Because it's new, there are no solutions on Stack Overflow. When the startup tries to hire more developers to help fix it, they realize *no one* in their city knows how to code in the obscure language. They have to rewrite the entire app. 

This is "Shiny Object Syndrome" (Resume-Driven Development).
Developers optimize the architecture for their own entertainment rather than business survival.

Elite engineering teams (Stripe, Basecamp) advocate for **Boring Technology** (PostgreSQL, Node.js, React). Battle-tested tech has infinite talent pools, zero "unknown unknowns", and allows you to focus 100% of your brain on building business features, not fighting the framework.

At Manifera, our Dutch Architects enforce extreme pragmatism. We do not allow our offshore Vietnamese pods to use experimental toys. We mandate universally standardized Boring Technology, ensuring your application is highly maintainable and your technical debt remains zero.
