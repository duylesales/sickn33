🌳 **A 'Big Bang' rewrite is an exercise in corporate arrogance.**

A CIO decides to modernize a 15-year-old legacy **technological software** platform. A consulting firm spends 18 months building the replacement in secret. 
They flip the switch on Monday morning. Total chaos. Customers are billed incorrectly and the servers crash. They are forced to roll back to the 15-year-old system. 

Why do Big Bang rewrites fail? Because legacy code contains decades of undocumented, hyper-specific business rules. A new team writing from scratch will mathematically miss those hidden rules. When they launch, the business processes break. 

Elite architects execute the **Strangler Fig Pattern**. 
You put an API Gateway in front of the old system. You rewrite one small feature at a time, route traffic to the new code for just that feature, and leave the rest of the old app running. You incrementally replace the architecture with zero downtime.
