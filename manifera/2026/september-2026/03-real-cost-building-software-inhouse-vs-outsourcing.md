A startup CEO recently told me: "We spent €240,000 building our engineering team last year. We shipped one feature." When I asked how much of that went to actual product development versus recruiting, onboarding, management overhead, and benefits — she could not answer.

This is the dirty secret of the "build in-house vs. outsource" debate: **most founders compare developer salaries against outsourcing invoices without accounting for the 40-60% overhead that makes in-house teams far more expensive than they appear on paper.**

## The Full Cost of an In-House Team (Netherlands)

Let us build a realistic cost model for a minimum viable engineering team in the Netherlands:

### The Team: 1 Tech Lead + 2 Senior Devs + 1 Mid Dev + 1 QA

| Cost Category | Annual Cost |
|--------------|-------------|
| Gross Salaries (5 engineers) | €380,000 |
| Employer Social Contributions (22%) | €83,600 |
| Health Insurance & Benefits | €25,000 |
| Office Space (Amsterdam, 5 desks) | €36,000 |
| Equipment (laptops, monitors, licenses) | €15,000 |
| Recruiting Costs (avg €8K per hire) | €40,000 (year 1) |
| Management Overhead (CTO time, 30%) | €45,000 |
| Onboarding & Ramp-up (3 months at 50% productivity) | €47,500 |
| **Total Year 1** | **€672,100** |
| **Total Year 2+** (no recruiting) | **€584,600** |

**Effective hourly cost per productive developer hour:** approximately **€78–€95/hour** when you account for holidays (25 days), sick leave (8 days), meetings (20% of time), and administrative overhead.

## The Full Cost of an Outsourced Team

Now, compare this to an equivalent team from a reputable offshore partner:

| Cost Category | Annual Cost |
|--------------|-------------|

| Communication tools (Slack, Jira, etc.) | €3,000 |
| Quarterly on-site visits (2 trips × €3,000) | €6,000 |

**Cost savings vs. in-house Year 1: €443,500 (66%)**
**Cost savings vs. in-house Year 2+: €356,000 (61%)**

## What the Spreadsheet Does Not Show

Cost is only half the equation. Here are the variables that spreadsheets miss:

### Speed to Market
An in-house team takes 3-5 months to assemble. An outsourced team can be operational in 2-4 weeks. For startups racing toward product-market fit, those 3 months of lost development time are worth more than any salary savings.

### Scaling Flexibility
If your product hits traction and you need to double the team, an outsourcing partner can add engineers within weeks. If traction stalls and you need to scale down, you reduce the team without severance packages, legal complications, or the emotional cost of layoffs.

### Institutional Knowledge Risk
This is where in-house teams genuinely win. When engineers leave an outsourced team, the partner manages the transition. But knowledge transfer is never perfect. The mitigation: **insist on comprehensive documentation, shared code repositories you own, and pair programming during onboarding.**

## The Hybrid Sweet Spot

The most capital-efficient model for startups between Seed and Series B is hybrid:

1. **Hire 1-2 in-house engineers** for architecture decisions, code review ownership, and stakeholder communication.
2. **Partner with an outsourcing firm** for the development capacity. This gives you scale without the fixed cost burden.
3. **Own all the code.** Your outsourcing contract must specify 100% IP transfer. A partner like [Manifera](https://www.manifera.com/services/custom-software-development/) operates under Dutch contract law, guaranteeing full code ownership from day one.

## When In-House Is the Right Call

Outsourcing is not always the answer. Build in-house when:
- Your core product **is** the technology (deep AI/ML, proprietary algorithms).
- Regulatory requirements demand on-premise engineers with security clearances.
- You have already achieved product-market fit and need a permanent, growing team.

## When Outsourcing Is the Right Call

Partner with an outsourced team when:
- You need to move from idea to MVP in under 3 months.
- Your runway is under 18 months and you cannot afford €600K+ in annual engineering costs.
- You need specific technical expertise (mobile, DevOps, AI integration) for a defined project scope.

## FAQ
### Does outsourcing mean lower quality code?
No. Code quality is a function of engineering culture — code reviews, CI/CD pipelines, automated testing — not geography. A well-structured offshore team with senior oversight will produce better code than an understaffed in-house team cutting corners to meet deadlines.

### How do I protect my intellectual property when outsourcing?
Three layers: (1) A contract under strong IP law (Dutch or UK law preferred) with explicit IP transfer clauses. (2) All code stored in your own repositories (GitHub/GitLab) that you control. (3) An NDA signed by every individual developer working on your project.

### What if my outsourcing partner goes out of business?
This is why code ownership and documentation matter. If you own the repository, the CI/CD configuration, and the deployment infrastructure, you can transition to a new partner or bring development in-house with minimal disruption.
