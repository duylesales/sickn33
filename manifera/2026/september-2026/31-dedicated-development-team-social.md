🚨 **Do not buy offshore coding capacity unless you are also buying the architectural governance to control it.**

A CTO hires an offshore **dedicated development team** (5 devs) to scale their SaaS platform. They treat the offshore devs like an internal team, giving them tickets and leaving them alone. 

Three months later, the codebase is a disaster. No automated tests, massive security flaws, and a monolithic database that cannot scale. The CTO fires them, blaming "low offshore quality."

The CTO is wrong. The failure was a Governance Failure.
Developers translate blueprints into code. Architects *design* the blueprint. If you give 5 mid-level developers no architectural blueprint and no strict Tech Lead to review their PRs, they will default to writing the fastest, sloppiest code possible. 

This is the Governance Illusion.
