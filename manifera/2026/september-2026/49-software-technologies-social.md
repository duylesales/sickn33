⏳ **Backend databases have a half-life of 20 years. Frontend JavaScript frameworks have a half-life of 3 years.**

A CTO allows their frontend team to build the UI using a trendy, experimental framework. Three years later, the framework's creators abandon it. There are no security updates, and no new developers know how to use it. 
The CTO is forced to halt product development for 6 months to execute a €500K rewrite into React.

This is the catastrophic cost of "JavaScript Fatigue."

When you let junior developers optimize the tech stack for their resumes (Shiny Object Syndrome), you are forced to become the unpaid beta-tester for untested **software technologies**. 

Elite architects survive this by implementing **API-First Decoupled Architecture**. The backend (Node.js/PostgreSQL) only outputs raw JSON. The frontend consumes it. Because they are decoupled, you can completely rewrite the UI in 5 years without ever touching the backend database.
