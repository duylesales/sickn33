⏰ **"90% done. For the third week in a row."**

If this sounds familiar, your offshore team doesn't have a people problem — it has a process problem. Here are the 5 root causes:

🔴 **#1: Ambiguous Acceptance Criteria**
"Build a dashboard" means different things in Amsterdam and Ho Chi Minh City.
→ FIX: Given/When/Then criteria + wireframes + edge cases for EVERY story.

🔴 **#2: The Estimation Gap**
Tasks estimated at 2 days take 5. Teams under-estimate to avoid looking slow.
→ FIX: Story points, not hours. 20% sprint buffer. Track actual vs. estimated over 4-6 sprints.

🔴 **#3: Silent Blockers**
Developer stuck for 2 days but doesn't mention it until sprint review.
→ FIX: "4-hour rule" — stuck for 4h? Flag it immediately. Create a safe culture for asking help.

🔴 **#4: Scope Creep via Slack**
PO sends "quick requests" directly to developers, bypassing the backlog.
→ FIX: ALL requests go through the backlog. PM protects sprint scope. Zero exceptions.

🔴 **#5: No CI/CD**
Features work alone, break when combined.
→ FIX: Automated tests on every PR. Merge to main daily. Deploy to staging every sprint.

These aren't offshore problems. They're process problems amplified by distance.
