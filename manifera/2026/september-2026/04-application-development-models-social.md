🏃 **The two-week Agile Sprint is destroying your codebase.**

For 15 years, Agile Scrum has been the standard **application development model**. But in complex B2B SaaS, the two-week sprint is a treadmill that forces engineers to make dangerous architectural compromises.

If an engineer hits a complex database problem on Thursday, they don't have time to fix it properly. They write a dirty "hack" just to close the ticket by Friday. The sprint looks successful, but you just absorbed massive technical debt.

*"Agile Scrum measures velocity. Elite engineering measures value."*

Elite teams are moving to the **Shape Up** methodology (by Basecamp):
✅ **6-Week Cycles:** Engineers have deep focus time to solve hard architectural problems. No Friday panic.
✅ **Fixed Time, Variable Scope:** The deadline is absolute, but engineers have autonomy to cut minor scope to hit the deadline safely.
✅ **2-Week Cooldown:** Time reserved for refactoring, fixing bugs, and curing burnout.
