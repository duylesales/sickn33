💸 **Building the MVP is 10% of the cost. Operating it is the other 90%.**

A startup founder has a €50K budget. They pay an agency €45K to **build an app**. It launches perfectly. 
Three months later, the startup is bankrupt. Why?
Because the AWS bill was €1,500/month. The agency charged €3,000 for a minor feature update. And the founder only had €5,000 left for marketing, resulting in zero paying customers.

This is the MVP Illusion. You didn't pay to build a business; you paid to build a liability on a server.

Founders must plan for the brutal reality of post-launch software economics:
✅ **Cost of Iteration:** Real users will hate 50% of your features. You need budget to rewrite them.
✅ **Cost of DevOps:** APIs deprecate and servers need security patches. Maintenance is mandatory.
✅ **Cost of Acquisition:** If you spend all your money on code, you can't afford to buy customers.
