☁️ **Moving a legacy monolith to AWS does not make it a "Cloud Application."**

When the board mandates a "Digital Transformation," many CTOs hire cheap agencies to execute a "Lift and Shift." The agency packages the massive legacy monolith into a virtual machine and puts it on AWS. 

Six months later, the CFO is furious. The monthly AWS bill is 300% higher than the old physical servers. Why? 

Because cloud pricing punishes static architecture. If you rent a 64-core server 24/7 just to handle a 10-minute CPU spike at midnight, you are burning capital.

To actually realize the financial benefits of **development in cloud**, you must transition to a Cloud-Native Architecture:
✅ **Microservices:** Scale only the components under load, not the entire application.
✅ **Managed Databases (DBaaS):** Stop paying engineers to patch OS vulnerabilities; let AWS RDS handle it.
✅ **Serverless Execution:** Move heavy background tasks to AWS Lambda. Pay for milliseconds of compute, not 24/7 idle servers.

*"Cloud computing is only cheaper if your architecture is elastic."*
