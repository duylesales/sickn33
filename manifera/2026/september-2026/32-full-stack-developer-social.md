🦄 **The Modern "Full Stack Developer" is a myth.**

A VP of Engineering hires 5 "Full Stack Developers" to build an enterprise platform. Six months later, it launches. 

The UI is slightly clunky, the animations stutter, and the database crashes under load. Why? Because the VP fell for the "Full Stack Fallacy."

In 2012, one brain could hold the entire stack. In modern enterprise architecture, it is neurologically impossible. 
A true Frontend Specialist must master React state management, SSR (Next.js), and browser memory. A true Backend Specialist must master concurrent DB design, message queues (Kafka), and strict CI/CD security.

*"A Full Stack Developer is usually a Backend Developer who writes terrible CSS, or a Frontend Developer who writes insecure database queries."*

Enterprise teams don't hire generalists. They hire T-Shaped specialists. 

At Manifera, we don't force our offshore developers to do everything. We staff our pods with deep Frontend Specialists and deep Backend Architects, governed by a Dutch Tech Lead.
