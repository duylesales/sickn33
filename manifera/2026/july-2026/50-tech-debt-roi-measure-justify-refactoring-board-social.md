When CTOs argue for "clean code," they lose. 
You must translate tech debt into the only language the board understands: **Financial Risk & Velocity Drag.**

**How to calculate the ROI of Refactoring:**

📉 **The Drag (Interest Rate):**
Your 10 devs cost €1M/year. 
They spend 35% of their time fighting legacy spaghetti code and regressions.
Cost of Tech Debt: **€350K/year.**

💡 **The Principal (Cost to Fix):**
A 1-month refactoring sprint for 5 devs costs ~€41K.

💰 **The ROI:**
If that sprint drops velocity drag from 35% to 15%, you recover €200K/year in engineering capacity.
Payback period? **~2.5 months.**

"CEO, if we invest 1 month into refactoring, we gain the output of 2 full-time developers for the rest of the year, without hiring."

**3 Rules for Tech Debt:**
1️⃣ The Hotspots (changes often, breaks often) → Refactor immediately.
2️⃣ The Sleeping Dogs (ugly code, but rarely touched) → Leave it alone. 0% interest rate.
3️⃣ The 20% Rule → Allocate 20% of every sprint to velocity/stability. Don't call it tech debt.

Professional engineers don't ask permission to write clean code. They embed it in the estimates.
