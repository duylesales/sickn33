📊 CEO: "How productive is our engineering team?"

If your CTO answers with "Lines of Code" or "Story Points," you have a problem.
LOC is an anti-metric. A brilliant engineer often deletes more code than they write to solve a problem.

In 2026, mature orgs use empirical frameworks: **DORA + SPACE.**

**DORA Metrics (Measures the Pipeline):**
🚀 Throughput: Deployment Frequency & Lead Time for Changes
🛡️ Stability: Change Failure Rate & MTTR (Mean Time to Recovery)

*Why DORA works:* It forces balance. If you try to code faster by skipping QA, your Change Failure Rate instantly spikes. You can't cheat the system.

**SPACE Framework (Measures the Human Element):**
DORA misses developer burnout. SPACE measures:
Satisfaction, Performance, Activity, Communication, and Efficiency/Flow.

If DORA "Lead Time" is taking 5 days, SPACE might reveal that the code was written in 1 day, but sat waiting for a PR review for 4 days due to meeting overload. The bottleneck isn't coding; it's async communication.

**The Golden Rules of Engineering Metrics:**
1️⃣ Automate collection (use LinearB or Jellyfish, don't use spreadsheets).
2️⃣ Measure TEAMS, never individuals.
3️⃣ Don't tie bonuses to these metrics (Goodhart's Law: when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure).

Measure pipeline flow. Protect developer focus time.
