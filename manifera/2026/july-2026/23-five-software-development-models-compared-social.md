🏗️ "We're Agile" = the new "We recycle"

Everyone claims it. Few do it properly.

Here's a no-BS comparison of 5 dev models that actually work in 2026:

**1. Waterfall** — Not dead. Just niche.
✅ Regulated/compliance-heavy projects
✅ Fixed-scope with clear requirements
❌ Anything that evolves (which is most software)
Success rate for €1M+ projects: 29%

**2. Agile Scrum** — The workhorse
✅ Product dev with evolving requirements
✅ Teams of 5-9 developers
❌ Teams >12 without SAFe framework
Gene Kim: "Scrum tells you how to organise work. Not how to do it well."

**3. Kanban** — The flow state
✅ Support/maintenance with unpredictable work
✅ Teams already delivering continuously
❌ Teams needing sprint discipline to stay focused

**4. DevOps / CD** — The speed machine
✅ SaaS needing daily deployments
✅ Mature testing culture
❌ Zero automated tests? This is chaos, not DevOps

**5. Shape Up** — The deep focus model
✅ 6-week cycles, no sprint ceremony overhead
✅ Product companies wanting autonomy
❌ Client projects with fixed scope

The truth? High-performing teams don't pick ONE model.

They combine Scrum's planning + DevOps' pipelines + Kanban's flow.

The model is a tool. Not a religion.
