🤖 AI tools promise "10x developers." 

The reality in enterprise software is different, but still massive.

Writing code is only 25% of a developer's job. AI doesn't 10x the developer; it 3x's the typing phase.

**The real metrics of AI-assisted dev in 2026:**
⚡ Feature boilerplate: +40-50% velocity
🛡️ Unit test generation: +60% coverage (with no extra sprint time)
🐛 Legacy bug tracing: -30% MTTR (Mean Time to Resolution)

**But unmanaged AI is dangerous.**
Beware the "Illusion of Competence." A junior dev prompts a complex multi-threaded database transaction. It compiles. They merge it. But they don't understand the memory leaks hidden inside.

Velocity goes up 📈
Stability crashes 📉

**The CTO Playbook for AI in 2026:**
1️⃣ Shift devs from "Code Writers" to "Code Reviewers". Scrutinize AI output like a PR from a junior.
2️⃣ Stricter CI/CD. If you generate code 50% faster, your automated safety nets (static analysis, linting) must be 50% stronger.
3️⃣ Standardize on Enterprise tools. No free ChatGPT pasting. Protect your IP.

AI won't replace your developers. But a team using AI will absolutely replace a team that isn't.
