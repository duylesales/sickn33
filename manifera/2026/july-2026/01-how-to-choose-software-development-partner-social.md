🔥 **Your software partner is your most important hire. Here's how to not screw it up.**

Every year, thousands of founders wire €30K to a dev company they found on Google — and end up with a half-broken prototype and ghosted Slack channels.

The 6 things I check before signing any contract:

1️⃣ **Process over portfolio** — A pretty case study page means nothing. Ask them to walk you through a sprint cycle. If they can't describe standups, reviews, and retros, they're winging it.

2️⃣ **Architecture test** — Ask for a high-level architecture sketch during the proposal phase. Free. If they can't do it, their "senior engineers" don't exist.

3️⃣ **IP ownership clause** — You must own 100% of your code. If the contract says "shared IP" or "licensed usage," RUN.

4️⃣ **Never pay >30% upfront** — The rest should be tied to verified, working deliverables.

5️⃣ **Timezone overlap > cheap hourly rate** — A 1-hour overlap = 23 hours of silence on critical questions.

6️⃣ **Ask for their unhappy clients** — "Can I speak to a client whose project didn't go perfectly?" Their answer tells you everything.

The best partnerships combine European business presence with offshore engineering talent. Best of both worlds.
