🧱 The job of a CTO is not to optimize for benchmark performance. 
The job is to optimize for long-term business survivability.

That means choosing **Boring Technology**.

"Boring" doesn't mean archaic. It means the failure modes are well-understood. 

PostgreSQL is boring. If it breaks at 3 AM, the solution is on StackOverflow in 10 seconds.
A nascent vector-graph NoSQL database with 400 GitHub stars is "exciting." When it corrupts data, you have to read the source code to figure out why.

**The 4 Pillars of Tech Stack Evaluation:**
1️⃣ **The Talent Pool:** Can I hire a mid-level dev in this language in 3 weeks? If no, don't use it.
2️⃣ **Dependency Risk:** Does Stripe have an official SDK for it? Or do I have to maintain my own wrapper?
3️⃣ **Innovation Tokens:** You only get 3. Spend them on your business model or AI features, not your database choice.
4️⃣ **AI Compatibility:** GitHub Copilot is trained on billions of lines of Java, TS, and Python. If you use an obscure language, you lose the massive AI productivity multiplier.

Innovate on the product. Be uncreative with the plumbing.
