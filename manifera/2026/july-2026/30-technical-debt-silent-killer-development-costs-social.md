**The 4 quadrants of technical debt:**

✅ Deliberate + Prudent — "Ship now, refactor after the deal closes"
❌ Deliberate + Reckless — "We don't have time for tests"
🤷 Inadvertent + Prudent — "Now we see a better approach"
💀 Inadvertent + Reckless — "What's a design pattern?"

Only quadrant 1 is acceptable. The rest are organisational problems.

**How a 30-minute shortcut becomes a 40-hour nightmare:**

Sprint 1: Hardcode a config value. Save 30 min.
Sprint 5: Pattern copied 12 times.
Sprint 12: Client needs a change. Find all 12 instances. Break 2. Fix for 10 hours.
Sprint 20: Nobody touches the module. It's now "legacy."

**The 20% rule (non-negotiable):**

Allocate 15-20% of every sprint to debt reduction.

Not a "tech debt sprint" that gets cancelled.
A permanent, embedded allocation.

**5 warning signs your debt is critical:**
📉 Feature velocity declining (same team, fewer points)
🐛 Bug fixes taking 3x longer than expected
⏰ Onboarding exceeds 3 months
🚫 Developers avoid certain modules
📦 Deployment frequency dropped to monthly

**The rewrite trap:** Starting over almost never works. Use the strangler fig pattern instead — replace piece by piece over 12-18 months.

Tech debt is a financial liability. Manage it like one.
