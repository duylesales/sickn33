🏢 **An enterprise project is NOT a startup MVP with a bigger budget.**

Most IT procurement teams evaluate dev vendors using startup criteria — then wonder why the partnership collapses 3 months in.

Here are the 8 non-negotiable capabilities that separate enterprise-ready dev firms from the rest:

1️⃣ **Security by architecture** — OWASP Top 10 baked in from commit #1, not bolted on before launch
2️⃣ **Compliance by design** — They should architect GDPR data deletion across microservices, not just check a legal box
3️⃣ **Integration engineering** — SAP, Salesforce, Azure AD, legacy databases. Have they done it before?
4️⃣ **Multi-tenant architecture** — Database-per-tenant vs. row-level security? They should know the trade-offs cold
5️⃣ **Scalable infrastructure** — Kubernetes, auto-scaling, 99.9% uptime, disaster recovery
6️⃣ **Change management** — Feature flags, environment promotion (Dev→Staging→UAT→Prod), rollback capability
7️⃣ **Documentation culture** — ADRs, API docs, runbooks. If only THEY understand the system, that's vendor lock-in
8️⃣ **Contractual maturity** — SLAs with penalties, DPA, escrow, audit rights

💡 Pro tip: Ask them to produce a technical architecture proposal for your specific project. If they don't address security, compliance, and scalability unprompted — they're not enterprise-ready.
