When Herre Roelevink founded Manifera in 2014, he noticed something that still holds true today: non-technical founders with brilliant business ideas consistently lose €50,000-€200,000 because they skip the validation phase and jump straight to development. They hire a team, build for six months, launch to an audience that does not exist, and wonder what went wrong. This roadmap exists to prevent that.

## Phase 0: Validate Before You Build (Weeks 1-4)

Before writing a single line of code, you must prove that people will pay for your solution. Not "would consider using it" — will actually pay money.

**Validation methods that cost under €500:**

| Method | What It Proves | Cost | Time |
|--------|---------------|------|------|
| **Landing page + waitlist** | People search for this problem | €50-200 (domain + hosting) | 1 week |
| **Competitor analysis** | A market exists and has revenue | Free | 2 days |
| **10 customer interviews** | Real humans describe this pain | Free | 2 weeks |
| **Fake door test** | People click "Buy" (even if the product does not exist yet) | €200-500 (ads) | 1 week |
| **Manual service delivery** | You can solve the problem without software | Free | 2-4 weeks |

The last method is the most powerful and the least used. If your software will automate a process, do that process manually for 10 customers first. You will learn more about requirements in two weeks of manual service delivery than in two months of specification writing.

## Phase 1: Define Your MVP Scope (Weeks 5-6)

An MVP (Minimum Viable Product) is not a stripped-down version of your full vision. It is the smallest product that solves one core problem for one specific user.

**The MVP Scope Framework:**

Write down every feature you want. Then ruthlessly cut using this filter:

1. **Does this feature directly solve the core problem?** → If no, cut it.
2. **Will users pay for the product WITHOUT this feature?** → If yes, cut it.
3. **Can this feature be manually handled for the first 100 users?** → If yes, cut it from V1.

Most founders start with 40 features and end with 6. That is correct. Instagram launched with 3 features: take a photo, apply a filter, share it. Everything else came later.

**A practical example:**

*Full vision:* AI-powered inventory management with demand forecasting, supplier integration, multi-warehouse support, and real-time analytics dashboard.

*MVP:* Barcode scanning that updates inventory counts in real-time, with a simple list view. That is it.

## Phase 2: Choose Your Development Partner (Weeks 7-8)

You have three options. Each has specific conditions where it wins:

| Option | Best When | Cost Range | Timeline |
|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Technical co-founder** | You have equity to offer and need long-term technical leadership | Equity (20-50%) | Variable |
| **Freelancers** | Simple projects with clear scope, budget under €15,000 | €30-100/hour | 4-12 weeks |
| **Development company** | Complex products, need full team (frontend + backend + QA), want project management | €35-80/hour (offshore) | 8-20 weeks |

You get a team with project management, QA, and multiple developers — not a single freelancer who might disappear.

## Phase 3: Build the MVP (Weeks 9-20)

**The build process broken down:**

**Weeks 9-10: Discovery & Architecture**
- User flow diagrams
- Database schema design
- API architecture
- Technology stack selection
- Wireframes for all screens

**Weeks 11-14: Core Feature Development**
- Build the 3-6 core features
- Daily or weekly demos to verify alignment
- Automated tests for critical paths

**Weeks 15-17: Integration & Polish**
- Connect frontend to backend
- Payment integration (if applicable)
- Authentication and user management
- Basic error handling

**Weeks 18-20: QA & Launch Prep**
- Testing across devices and browsers
- Performance testing under load
- Security review
- App Store/Play Store submission (if mobile)
- Deployment to production

**Total MVP cost (realistic ranges):**

| Complexity | Features | Cost (Offshore) | Cost (Western EU) |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Simple** (landing page + 1 core workflow) | 3-5 | €15,000 - €35,000 | €40,000 - €80,000 |
| **Medium** (multi-role app with API integrations) | 6-10 | €35,000 - €70,000 | €80,000 - €160,000 |
| **Complex** (real-time features, AI components) | 10-15 | €70,000 - €150,000 | €160,000 - €350,000 |

## Phase 4: Launch and Learn (Weeks 21-24)

Your launch is not a press release. It is a controlled experiment.

**Week 21:** Soft launch to 20-50 users from your waitlist. Watch them use the product. Take notes on every point of confusion.

**Week 22:** Fix the top 5 usability issues. Do not add features — fix friction.

**Week 23:** Expand to 100-200 users. Measure: activation rate, retention at day 7, and willingness to pay.

**Week 24:** Decide: iterate, pivot, or scale. If day-7 retention exceeds 40% and 3+ users have paid, you have signal. Scale development. If retention is below 20%, talk to churned users. The product may need repositioning, not more features.

## The Five Mistakes That Burn the Most Money

1. **Building before validating.** €50,000 wasted on average.
2. **Over-specifying the MVP.** Every unnecessary feature costs €5,000-€15,000 and delays launch by 2-4 weeks.
3. **Choosing the cheapest developer.** The €15/hour developer who takes 6 months costs more than the €50/hour developer who delivers in 10 weeks.
4. **Ignoring mobile.** 72% of B2C SaaS usage is mobile. If your MVP is desktop-only, you are testing on the wrong audience.
5. **No analytics from day one.** If you cannot measure user behaviour, you cannot learn. Install analytics before launch, not after.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much money should I raise before starting development? (Scenario: First-time founder preparing a seed round pitch)

For an offshore-developed MVP, budget €40,000-€80,000 in development costs plus €10,000-€20,000 for go-to-market (design, domain, marketing, legal). Add a 25% buffer for unexpected scope changes. So a realistic seed budget for an MVP launch is €60,000-€125,000. Pre-seed rounds in Europe average €150,000-€500,000, which provides comfortable runway.

### Can I build an MVP without any technical knowledge? (Scenario: Business consultant with a SaaS idea and no coding experience)

Yes, but you need to invest time in understanding basics: what frontend and backend mean, what an API does, how databases store data. You do not need to code — you need to communicate effectively with developers. Spend one weekend watching a "How the internet works" course on YouTube. This investment saves thousands in miscommunication during development.

### How do I protect my idea when working with a development company? (Scenario: Founder worried about IP theft by an offshore team)

Three mechanisms: (1) NDA signed before sharing any details. (2) IP assignment clause in the development contract — all code, designs, and documentation are your property. (3) Code hosted in YOUR repository (GitHub, GitLab) from day one, not the vendor's. Manifera's contracts include full IP assignment as standard.

### Should I build a mobile app or a web app for my MVP? (Scenario: B2B SaaS founder unsure about platform choice)

For B2B SaaS, start with a web app. Web apps are faster and cheaper to build, easier to update, and do not require app store approval. Build mobile only when you have validated demand and need device-specific features (camera, GPS, push notifications). For B2C products with heavy mobile usage, consider a Progressive Web App as a middle ground.

### When should I hire my first in-house developer vs. continuing with an outsourced team? (Scenario: Post-MVP founder with growing traction)

Hire your first in-house developer when you have: (1) Product-market fit confirmed (>40% day-7 retention, paying customers). (2) Revenue or runway to sustain a 12-month salary commitment. (3) A technical roadmap that requires daily collaboration impossible to achieve across time zones. Until then, a dedicated offshore team is more cost-effective and flexible.
