🗺️ The most expensive mistake in software isn't a buggy release. 

It is flawlessly executing a 6-month development cycle to build a product that nobody wants. 

47% of failed software projects fail due to poor requirements. Rushing to write code because it feels like "progress" is a trap.

In 2026, premium dev agencies refuse to code on Day 1. They require a **Product Discovery Phase** (2-4 weeks).

**The 4 Deliverables you MUST demand before coding starts:**
1️⃣ User Journey Maps (exposes massive edge cases early)
2️⃣ Clickable Figma Prototypes (test on users with zero code)
3️⃣ Architecture Blueprint (prevents catastrophic DB migrations in year 2)
4️⃣ A Ruthlessly Prioritized MVP Backlog

Fixing a UX flaw in a Figma prototype takes 10 minutes. 
Fixing that same flaw after it's coded takes 10 days of engineering time. 

Think of Discovery (usually 10-15% of your budget) as an insurance policy against building the wrong product.

Build the right thing, before you build it right.
