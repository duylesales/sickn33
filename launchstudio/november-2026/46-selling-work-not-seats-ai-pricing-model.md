# Selling Work, Not Seats: The AI Pricing Model
For twenty years, the B2B SaaS industry was built on the "Per Seat" pricing model. You built a tool, and you charged the enterprise $50 per month for every human employee who needed a login. Generative AI fundamentally breaks this economic model. If your AI product is highly effective, it allows the enterprise to reduce its human headcount. If you charge per human seat, making your AI better actually decreases your revenue. Startups must pivot from renting tools to **Selling Work**.

## The Flaw of Seat-Based Pricing in AI

Imagine a traditional Customer Support software company that charges $50/month per human support agent. A client has 100 agents, generating $5,000/month in revenue.

The software company introduces a brilliant AI feature that resolves 80% of tickets automatically. The client realizes they no longer need 100 agents; they only need 20 humans to handle the edge cases. They fire 80 people. The software company's revenue instantly drops from $5,000 to $1,000. By delivering massive value, the SaaS company destroyed its own business model.

## Usage-Based Pricing (The API Model)

The first step away from "Seats" is **Usage-Based Pricing**. Instead of charging for human logins, you charge for compute.

You charge $0.05 per AI-generated email, or $1.00 per hour of AI voice generation. This aligns your revenue with the client's actual utilization. If the client fires their human support staff and relies entirely on your AI, your AI usage skyrockets, and your revenue increases proportionally. However, Usage-Based pricing still treats AI as a utility, not a premium service.

## Outcome-Based Pricing (The Digital Labor Model)

The ultimate evolution is **Outcome-Based Pricing**. Corporate buyers do not want to buy "tokens" or "compute hours"; they want to buy completed business objectives.

If you build an AI Sales Development Agent (SDR), you do not charge $500/month for the software. You charge $50 for every verified meeting the AI successfully books on the client's calendar. You are pricing the AI exactly like a freelance contractor. You are selling the outcome (the meeting), absorbing the compute cost yourself, and capturing a massive premium for the guaranteed result.

## Value-Share (The Commission Model)

For highly advanced, vertically integrated AI products, startups are moving to pure **Value-Share** models.

An AI debt-collection startup might charge zero upfront fees. Instead, the AI autonomously negotiates with debtors via text and voice. If the AI successfully recovers $10,000 in bad debt, the startup takes a 15% commission ($1,500). This perfectly aligns the incentives of the startup and the client, completely bypassing the traditional SaaS procurement budget and tapping directly into the client's profit margins.

## Key Takeaways

- Charging 'per user' is dead. Old software charged $50 for every human who logged in. But if your AI does the work of 10 humans, the company will fire those 10 people. If you charge per human, you lose money when your AI works well!

- Stop renting tools; sell labor. You are not selling a software dashboard anymore. You are renting out a digital employee. You must price your software the same way you pay a freelancer.

- Charge per 'Outcome'. Don't charge a flat monthly fee. If your AI writes legal documents, charge $20 for every document it finishes. If it does zero work, it costs zero. If it writes 1,000 documents, you make massive profits.

- Take a commission. If your AI bot successfully closes a $10,000 sales deal for your client, don't charge a $50 software fee. Take a 10% commission on the sale. Your software is now a revenue partner, not a cost center.

- Make the client rich. In the 'Outcome' model, the client only pays you when the AI successfully makes them money (or saves them time). This makes the software incredibly easy to sell because there is zero financial risk for the buyer.

## FAQ

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is 'Per Seat' pricing?

This is how old software companies make money. They charge $50 per month for every human employee ('seat') that needs a login to the system. If you hire 10 new people, your software bill goes up.

### Why does AI kill the 'Seat' model?

Because AI replaces the humans. If your AI automation allows a company to fire 10 people, they now have 10 fewer 'seats'. If you charge per seat, you actually lose money by making your software better.

### What is Outcome-Based Pricing?

Instead of charging for a login, you charge for a completed task. If your AI writes legal contracts, you don't charge $50 a month for access. You charge $10 for every completed, verified contract the AI writes.

### Is this like hiring a freelancer?

Exactly. You are no longer selling 'software'; you are selling 'digital labor'. The AI acts as an employee, and you are charging a 'piece-rate' salary for the work it does.

### What is 'Value-Based' pricing?

It is the ultimate pricing model. If your AI Sales Agent successfully closes a $100,000 deal for your client, you don't charge a flat fee. You take a 10% commission ($10,000). Your revenue is tied directly to the value you created.
