## The Problem with In-House Engineering

When an agency tries to pivot into custom software development by hiring in-house developers, three things usually go wrong:

### 1. The Cost of Talent
To build secure, scalable B2B SaaS, you cannot hire junior developers. You need a Senior Backend Architect, a DevOps Specialist, and a React expert. In Europe, that team will cost you over €250,000 annually. If your agency doesn't have a constant, 12-month pipeline of massive software projects, that payroll will destroy your margins.

### 2. The Liability Trap
Enterprise AI software requires strict security. If an in-house developer accidentally exposes an OpenAI API key or leaks a client's private database because they didn't understand Row Level Security (RLS), your agency is legally liable. 

### 3. Distraction from Core Competencies
Your agency is great at UX design, strategy, and client relations. Managing Jira sprints, conducting code reviews, and maintaining server infrastructure is a completely different business model that will distract you from what you do best.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What exactly is White-Label Engineering?
White-label engineering is a B2B service where a specialized software firm (LaunchStudio) builds custom software on behalf of another company (your agency), completely unbranded. Your agency presents the final product to the client as your own work.

### How does the pricing and profit work?
We provide you with a fixed-price quote based on the technical requirements (e.g., €20,000). You add your agency's markup, design fees, and management fees, and sell it to the client (e.g., €50,000). You keep the margin, and you only pay us when the client pays you.

### Will LaunchStudio try to steal my clients?
Absolutely not. We sign strict Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and Non-Compete clauses. Our entire business model relies on making agencies successful so they bring us more projects. Stealing a client would destroy our reputation. We operate entirely invisibly under your brand.

### Do you handle ongoing maintenance after the app launches?
Yes. We offer white-labeled Service Level Agreements (SLAs). You charge your client a monthly retainer for "App Maintenance & Hosting," and you pay us a flat fee to actually execute the 24/7 server monitoring, dependency updates, and bug fixes. 

### Can your engineers join our client calls?
Yes. For complex enterprise projects, a LaunchStudio technical lead can join your client pitches or technical due diligence meetings. We use an email address from your agency's domain and introduce ourselves as your "Lead Backend Architect."
