# Affiliate Marketing for AI SaaS: Building a Commission-Only Sales Team

If you have achieved Product-Market Fit (PMF) and your infrastructure is stable, your only goal is distribution. But hiring a sales team is expensive, and running paid ads is a gamble. The most capital-efficient growth engine in SaaS is an Affiliate Program. You essentially build an army of marketers who work for free, and you only pay them when they deliver a paying customer.

## The Economics of Affiliate SaaS

Traditional affiliate programs (like Amazon Associates) offer a 3% commission on physical goods. Software margins are entirely different. Because the marginal cost of a new SaaS user is near zero, you can afford to be extraordinarily aggressive.

The gold standard for AI wrappers is a **30% recurring commission for 12 months**. If your software costs $30/month, the affiliate makes $9 every month that user stays active. This recurring model is highly attractive to professional content creators and incentivizes them to create dedicated, high-quality tutorial videos for your product.

## Setting up the Infrastructure

Do not try to build a custom referral tracker in Supabase. Managing tracking cookies, handling refunds, and preventing fraud will consume weeks of engineering time.

Use platforms like **Rewardful** or **PartnerStack**. They integrate directly with your existing Stripe account in minutes. They provide you with a portal to accept affiliates, and they provide the affiliates with a dashboard to grab their custom links and track their payouts. When a user buys via Stripe, the platform automatically splits the revenue.

## Phase 1: Turning Users into Evangelists

Your first affiliates should be your happiest users. In your app's dashboard, add a prominent button: *"Get Your Next Month Free."*

Link this to their auto-generated affiliate portal. Explain the math simply: "Refer 3 friends, and your subscription is completely covered." This turns your software into a viral loop, where users actively market your tool in their private Slack channels and LinkedIn networks to subsidize their own costs.

## Phase 2: Recruiting Professional Marketers

Once the system works for users, you recruit the professionals: niche bloggers, newsletter writers, and YouTubers.

**The Pitch**: "I saw you wrote a review on [Competitor AI Tool]. We built a faster alternative, and we offer a 40% recurring commission (double what they offer). Here is a free premium account to test it. If you add us to your 'Top 10 AI Tools' list, it could be a significant new revenue stream for your site."

You only need 5 to 10 high-traffic bloggers to rank you #1 on their listicles to completely transform your inbound traffic pipeline.

## Protecting Your Brand (The Rules)

Affiliate marketing has a dark side: spam and brand cannibalization. You must set strict Terms of Service before launching the program.

**The Most Important Rule: No Brand Bidding.**

If your company is called "LaunchStudio," an affiliate might buy Google Ads for the keyword "LaunchStudio." When a user searches for you, they click the affiliate's ad instead of your organic link, and you end up paying a 30% commission for a user who was already looking for you. You must explicitly ban bidding on your branded keywords and ban spamming social media comments.

## Key Takeaways

- Affiliate marketing acts as a commission-only sales team; you only pay when a customer's credit card is successfully charged.

- Offer aggressive commissions (30%-40% recurring for the first year) to attract high-quality creators and bloggers.

- Use third-party platforms like Rewardful to integrate with Stripe, eliminating the need to build complex tracking software.

- Turn your existing users into your first affiliates by offering them a way to cover their subscription costs through referrals.

- Establish strict rules prohibiting affiliates from buying ads on your branded keywords to prevent them from stealing your organic traffic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How does an affiliate program work for SaaS?

Third parties promote your software using tracking links. If someone clicks their link and buys, the affiliate receives a percentage of that revenue. It is a zero-risk marketing channel.

### How much commission should I offer affiliates?

SaaS margins allow for aggressive payouts. The industry standard for AI tools is offering a 20% to 40% recurring commission for the first year of the customer's subscription.

### How do I track affiliate sales?

Use established platforms like Rewardful or PartnerStack. They integrate directly with Stripe, automatically track referrals, and handle the complex math of recurring payouts and refunds.

### Should I let my existing users become affiliates?

Yes. Turning users into advocates creates a viral loop. If users know they can pay for their subscription by referring colleagues, they will market the tool for you.
