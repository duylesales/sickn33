## What Makes an AI Software Developer Different

A traditional developer receives requirements, designs a solution, and writes code from scratch. Their workflow assumes a blank canvas.

An AI software developer receives an existing AI-generated codebase, understands its patterns and limitations, and builds production infrastructure around it. Their workflow assumes a populated canvas with specific structural conventions.

The skills required are surprisingly different:

| Skill | Traditional Developer | AI Software Developer |
|---|---|---|
| Read AI-generated code patterns | Rarely encountered | Daily practice |
| Identify what to keep vs. rebuild | Not applicable | Core competency |
| Work with Supabase/Firebase defaults | One option among many | Primary database pattern |
| Understand Lovable React conventions | Unknown | Familiar |
| Preserve frontend during backend rebuild | Unusual request | Standard process |
| Estimate work on AI codebases | Guessing | Calibrated from experience |

When you hire a traditional developer to work on your AI-generated prototype, they typically react in one of three ways:

**Reaction 1: "This needs to be rewritten."** They are uncomfortable with code they did not write, especially code that follows AI-specific conventions they have never seen. Their instinct is to start fresh in patterns they know.

**Reaction 2: "I'll fix it, but it will take a while."** They accept the codebase but spend weeks understanding patterns that an AI software developer would recognize instantly. Learning time becomes billable hours.

**Reaction 3: "I can work with this."** Rare. These are developers who have already encountered AI-generated code and know which parts are solid and which need replacement.

## How to Evaluate Any Developer Working on AI Code

Whether you hire internally, freelance, or use LaunchStudio, evaluate your AI software developer with these five questions:

1. **"Show me a Lovable/Bolt/Cursor project you took to production."** If they cannot show one, they will learn on your project.

2. **"What would you keep and what would you replace in this codebase?"** A good AI software developer preserves the frontend and replaces backend infrastructure. A bad one replaces everything.

3. **"How would you handle Supabase RLS for this use case?"** This tests whether they understand the most common database security gap in AI-generated code.

4. **"What is your approach to preserving AI-readability after modifications?"** The best AI software developers write code that remains compatible with Lovable/Cursor, so you can keep iterating with AI tools after launch.

5. **"Give me a fixed price and timeline."** Developers who understand AI codebases can estimate accurately. Those who are guessing will hedge with hourly rates and open-ended timelines.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### (Scenario: Founder about to hire their first developer) How do I find a developer who understands AI-generated code?

Look for specific experience with Lovable, Bolt, or Cursor codebases — not just general AI tool usage. Ask for portfolio examples of AI-generated projects taken to production. If you cannot find individual developers, consider LaunchStudio, whose entire engineering team specializes in AI-generated code production-hardening.

### (Scenario: Founder who already hired a developer who wants to rebuild everything) My developer says my AI code needs to be completely rewritten. Is that true?

In 90% of cases, no. AI-generated frontends (React components, routing, styling) are production-quality. What needs replacing is the backend infrastructure — database access patterns, security, payment processing. A developer who insists on rebuilding the frontend likely does not understand AI-generated code patterns.

### (Scenario: CTO evaluating in-house vs. outsourced AI development) Should I build an in-house AI software development team or outsource?

At the pre-seed and seed stage, outsource production engineering to LaunchStudio (€800–€7,500 per project). Start building in-house when you have consistent development needs and Series A funding. Manifera can also provide dedicated team engagement for scale-up stage companies needing ongoing engineering.

### (Scenario: Founder comparing developer hourly rates) Why does LaunchStudio use fixed pricing instead of hourly rates?

Hourly rates incentivize developers to take longer. Fixed pricing incentivizes efficiency. Because LaunchStudio's team works with AI-generated codebases daily, they can accurately estimate scope and commit to fixed prices. This protects founders from the cost overruns that plague hourly engagements.

### (Scenario: Developer curious about transitioning to AI software development) How can traditional developers upskill to become AI software developers?

Build five projects using Lovable, Bolt, and Cursor. Then production-harden each one — add proper security, payments, and deployment. This hands-on experience teaches you the patterns, gaps, and infrastructure needs that define AI software development. Manifera actively recruits developers with this combined skillset.
