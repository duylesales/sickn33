"How can one company charge €800 and another charge €80,000 for what sounds like the same thing — building software?" This question, asked by nearly every founder comparing quotes for the first time, has a real answer, and it isn't that one price is a scam. The two numbers describe genuinely different scopes of work.

## What €800–€7,500 (LaunchStudio) Actually Covers

This price range assumes you've already built a working prototype using an AI tool — Lovable, Bolt, Cursor, or v0 — that includes your core feature logic and user interface. The engineering work covers the "last mile": security hardening, authentication, database configuration, payment integration, hosting, and deployment. Critically, your frontend design work is already done; this scope doesn't include designing or building it from scratch.

## What €80,000+ (Traditional Custom Development) Actually Covers

This price range typically starts from zero: product discovery, UX/UI design, frontend development, backend architecture, database design, and the same last-mile infrastructure work LaunchStudio provides — all built from a blank canvas rather than an existing AI-generated foundation. A traditional agency quoting €80,000 is pricing in months of design and frontend engineering that an AI-native founder using Lovable or Bolt has already effectively completed themselves, for free or near-free.

## Why Neither Price Is "Wrong"

A traditional agency charging €80,000 for a project that genuinely starts from a blank page, with no existing design or prototype, is not overpricing — that scope of work has always cost roughly that much, because it requires months of skilled human labor across design, frontend, and backend disciplines. What has changed is that AI tools have made the discovery-through-prototype phase dramatically cheaper for founders willing to do it themselves, which shrinks the remaining scope — and therefore the remaining cost — to a fraction of the traditional total.

## Where the Confusion Comes From

Founders comparing these two prices are often, without realizing it, comparing a partial scope (last-mile only) against a full scope (everything from discovery to launch). The €800–€7,500 range only makes sense — and only represents good value — if you've already done the design and prototyping work an AI tool enables. A founder with just an idea and no prototype is not LaunchStudio's target customer; that founder needs the full-scope engagement Manifera's broader development services provide.

## Real example

### An AI-Native Founder in Action: Comparing Three Quotes That Weren't Actually Comparable

Lars, a construction project manager in Den Bosch, built BouwPlanner, an AI scheduling tool that helped small construction subcontractors coordinate multi-trade project timelines, using Lovable. Ready to launch, he requested quotes from three sources: a large Amsterdam digital agency, a freelance developer, and LaunchStudio.

The agency quoted €65,000 and four months, proposing to "properly rebuild" BouwPlanner with a dedicated UX research phase — despite Lars having already validated his interface design with eleven subcontractors who loved it. The freelancer quoted €12,000 with a vague scope and no fixed timeline. LaunchStudio quoted €3,400 for a Launch & Grow package, scoped specifically around what BouwPlanner already had versus what it needed: authentication, database security, Mollie billing, and hosting.

Lars initially found the price gap confusing until a call with LaunchStudio's team explicitly walked through what each quote actually included. The agency's quote covered work Lars had already completed himself through Lovable prototyping and subcontractor feedback. LaunchStudio's quote covered only the remaining, genuinely necessary infrastructure work.

**Result:** BouwPlanner launched in three weeks at roughly 5% of the agency's quote, with the exact interface Lars's subcontractor testers had already validated and approved, fully production-ready with security and billing intact.

> *"Once someone explained that the €65,000 quote included redesigning something I'd already built and tested, the price difference made complete sense. I wasn't buying a cheaper version of the same thing — I was buying only what I actually still needed."*
> — **Lars Bosman, Founder, BouwPlanner (Den Bosch)**

## Frequently Asked Questions

### If LaunchStudio is so much cheaper, is the quality lower than a traditional agency?

No — the quality standard is the same or higher in the areas both scopes cover (security, architecture, payments), since LaunchStudio is backed by Manifera's 11+ years of engineering experience serving enterprise clients like Vodafone and TNO. The price difference reflects scope (what's included), not a quality discount.

### How do I know if I actually need the full traditional agency scope instead of LaunchStudio's last-mile scope?

If you don't yet have a working prototype with a validated interface — just an idea or rough sketches — you likely need the fuller scope of discovery, design, and frontend development that a traditional engagement or Manifera's broader services provide. If you already have a working AI-generated prototype, LaunchStudio's scope is usually the right fit.

### Why doesn't LaunchStudio just charge more since founders would pay agency prices anyway?

LaunchStudio's pricing reflects the actual engineering time required for the last-mile scope, not what the market might bear if founders were unaware of the AI-native alternative. Transparent, fixed pricing tied to real scope is a deliberate positioning choice, reinforced by the price calculator being public rather than requiring a sales negotiation.

### Can a project start at LaunchStudio's price range and grow into needing Manifera's full-scope services later?

Yes, this is a common and natural progression. A founder might launch with LaunchStudio's last-mile scope, grow the business, and later need broader custom development — new major features, additional platforms, larger team support — which Manifera's full-cycle development services are built to handle.

### Is it ever worth paying agency prices even if I already have an AI-generated prototype?

In rare cases, if your prototype's design genuinely doesn't work for your market and needs a fundamental UX rethink beyond what incremental changes can fix. For most founders whose prototype interface tested well with real users, as with Lars's subcontractor feedback, preserving that validated design and only paying for the remaining infrastructure is the more capital-efficient choice.
