Investors have by now seen hundreds of impressive AI demos. A polished AI-generated prototype demo, once a genuine differentiator, has become table stakes — nearly every founder pitching in 2027 can produce one, because the tools that make it possible are the same tools every other founder has access to. What actually differentiates a fundable pitch now is evidence the demo represents something real.

## Why "It Works in the Demo" No Longer Impresses Investors

Sophisticated investors have specifically learned to discount polished AI demos, because they've seen enough of them to know demo quality has become decoupled from underlying business viability — a beautiful Lovable-generated interface says nothing about whether real customers want the product, will pay for it, or will stick around. The signal-to-noise ratio of demos has collapsed precisely because AI tools made demos cheap to produce for everyone.

## What Actually Signals Fundability Now

### Real, Paying Customers — Even a Small Number
A handful of customers who found your product, signed up, and paid real money is a categorically stronger signal than any number of demo walkthroughs, because it required your product to survive contact with the real world: real payment processing, real onboarding, real usage.

### Retention and Usage Data
Investors increasingly want to see actual usage patterns — are customers coming back, using the product regularly, expanding usage over time — data that simply doesn't exist for a prototype that's never been exposed to real, ongoing usage.

### Evidence the Infrastructure Can Scale
A live product that has handled real concurrent users without falling over demonstrates operational credibility a prototype cannot — investors specifically probing technical due diligence will ask about security, data handling, and reliability in ways a "we haven't launched yet" answer doesn't satisfy.

### A Founder Who Has Navigated Real Operational Challenges
Founders who've handled a real customer support issue, a real payment failure, or a real security question bring a credibility to the pitch that founders describing hypothetical future scenarios cannot match.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many paying customers do I realistically need before a pitch to make a meaningful difference?

There's no universal threshold, but even a small number (10-20) of genuine paying customers with reasonable retention, as in Marit's case, meaningfully changes the investor conversation compared to zero real customers — the qualitative shift from "hypothetical" to "demonstrated" matters more than the specific quantity at early stages.

### Is it worth delaying a fundraise to get a product live first, or should I pitch the prototype now and iterate later?

This depends on your specific timeline pressure and runway, but if you can realistically launch within a few weeks (as LaunchStudio's typical timeline allows), the resulting evidence often significantly improves pitch outcomes enough to justify a short delay, as illustrated by Marit's experience.

### Do investors specifically ask about technical infrastructure and security during due diligence?

Increasingly yes, particularly as AI-generated prototypes with weak security have become a known category of risk investors have learned to probe for. Being able to confidently describe your production security posture, rather than an uncertain "we haven't gotten to that yet," meaningfully strengthens technical due diligence conversations.

### Can LaunchStudio help prepare technical materials for investor due diligence specifically?

The team can provide documentation of the production architecture and security measures implemented, which founders can incorporate into their own due diligence materials, though the broader fundraising narrative and materials remain the founder's responsibility.

### Does having Manifera's name associated with a founder's technical infrastructure carry weight with investors?

It can provide supporting credibility, particularly given Manifera's track record with recognizable enterprise clients like Vodafone and TNO, though the primary driver of investor confidence remains the founder's own demonstrated traction and business fundamentals, with technical credibility as a supporting factor.
