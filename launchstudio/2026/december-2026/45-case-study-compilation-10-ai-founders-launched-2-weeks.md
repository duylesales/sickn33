Tien verschillende founders. Tien verschillende sectoren. Eén gedeeld patroon: een AI-prototype, een harde deadline, en minder dan twee weken tot een veilig, betaalklaar product. Two weeks sounds aggressive until you see it happen repeatedly, across industries with nothing in common except the same underlying gap between AI-generated prototype and production reality.

## Ten Founders, Ten Industries, One Pattern

**1. Sam (Ede) — Veterinary supply ordering tool.** Built with Bolt for local veterinary clinics to reorder consumables automatically based on AI-predicted usage patterns. Launched with Mollie billing and multi-clinic data isolation in 9 business days.

**2. Loes (Veenendaal) — AI meal-prep planning for elderly home care.** A Lovable prototype generating dietary-restriction-aware weekly meal plans for home care organizations. Production launch added healthcare-compliant data storage and secure hosting in 11 business days.

**3. Twan (Purmerend) — Freight documentation AI assistant.** Built with Cursor to auto-generate customs and shipping documentation drafts for small import/export businesses. Authentication and audit-trail logging added in 8 business days.

**4. Anouk (Hoorn) — AI-powered wedding vendor matching.** A v0-generated matchmaking tool connecting couples with local wedding vendors based on style preferences. Payment escrow logic and vendor onboarding flow completed in 13 business days.

**5. Guus (Bergen op Zoom) — Agricultural equipment maintenance predictor.** Built with Bolt for small farms to predict equipment maintenance needs from usage logs. Database security and offline-capable sync added in 10 business days.

**6. Marloes (Oss) — AI dental appointment triage.** A Lovable tool that used patient-described symptoms to suggest appointment urgency for a small dental practice group. Healthcare data compliance and Mollie billing completed in 12 business days.

**7. Stefan (Kampen) — Bicycle repair shop diagnostic assistant.** Built with Cursor to help repair shop staff diagnose issues from customer-described symptoms and photos. Multi-location data isolation and secure hosting added in 9 business days.

**8. Yvonne (Waalwijk) — Children's shoe sizing AI tool for independent retailers.** A v0 prototype using photo analysis to recommend shoe sizes for online orders. Payment integration and inventory sync completed in 11 business days.

**9. Bart (Barneveld) — Poultry farm compliance reporting assistant.** Built with Bolt to generate regulatory compliance reports from farm sensor data. Secure data pipeline and audit logging added in 14 business days.

**10. Wendy (Katwijk) — AI-powered fishing charter booking assistant.** A Lovable tool matching customers with available charter boats based on preferences and weather conditions. Payment processing and cancellation policy logic completed in 9 business days.

## What These Ten Founders Had in Common

None of these ten businesses have anything to do with each other on the surface — veterinary supplies, wedding vendors, poultry compliance, fishing charters. What they shared was the exact same underlying gap: a working, AI-generated prototype with zero production infrastructure, and a real deadline (a customer commitment, a seasonal window, a trade event) forcing the last-mile work to happen fast, correctly, without disturbing the interface each founder had already designed.

## Real example

### An AI-Native Founder in Action: The Eleventh Story, Told in Full

Beyond the ten summarized above, one launch from this same period deserves the fuller telling. Ivo, a viticulture hobbyist who had turned a small vineyard consulting side business in Wageningen into his full-time work, built WijngaardAdvies, an AI tool that analyzed soil sensor data and weather patterns to generate irrigation and treatment recommendations for small Dutch vineyard owners, using Lovable.

Ivo had a specific, immovable deadline: the annual Dutch Wine Growers conference, six weeks away, where he'd secured a demo slot in front of exactly the audience of independent vineyard owners who were his target customers. His Lovable prototype produced genuinely useful recommendations in testing, but had no real user accounts (every vineyard's data was mixed together in one shared view), no way to charge for the seasonal subscription he'd planned, and ran only from his laptop with no public hosting at all.

Ivo contacted LaunchStudio nine days before the conference, having spent the prior weeks on marketing preparation and only belatedly realizing the technical gap. The Manifera team prioritized ruthlessly against the hard deadline: per-vineyard data isolation, secure hosting on a real domain, and a simplified Mollie payment flow covering the seasonal subscription model — deliberately deferring lower-priority polish items to after the conference.

**Result:** WijngaardAdvies went live four days before the conference, giving Ivo time to test the live product himself before demoing it. He signed 14 vineyard owners to paid seasonal subscriptions directly at the conference — each able to create their own account and see their own data live, on the spot, during his demo.

> *"Nine days felt impossible when I called. LaunchStudio didn't try to do everything — they asked me exactly what the conference demo actually needed to prove, and built precisely that, in time, with room to spare for me to test it myself first."*
> — **Ivo Bakker, Founder, WijngaardAdvies (Wageningen)**

**Cost & Timeline:** €2,900 (Launch & Grow Package, conference-deadline priority) — live in 5 business days, 4 days ahead of the conference.
