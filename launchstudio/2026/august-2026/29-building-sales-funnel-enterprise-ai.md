# Building a Sales Funnel for Enterprise AI Software - AI For Software Engineering
Self-serve SaaS ("Product-Led Growth") works brilliantly when you are selling a $29/month AI image generator to freelancers. It fails spectacularly when you are selling a $50,000/year autonomous data extraction pipeline to a multinational logistics firm. Enterprise sales require a fundamentally different architecture: Sales-Led Growth. Here is how to build a B2B AI sales funnel that converts massive contracts.

## The 'Book a Demo' Gateway

The enterprise funnel begins by aggressively gating your highest tier. Your pricing page should list "Starter" ($49/mo) and "Pro" ($199/mo) with self-serve Stripe checkout buttons. But the "Enterprise" tier must have a **"Book a Demo"** or **"Talk to Sales"** button. Do not list a price. Price is determined by the value you provide, and you cannot determine the value until you talk to them.

When they click the button, route them to a specialized lead-capture form. Ask qualifying questions: *"How many employees are in your organization?"* If the answer is "2," route them to a pre-recorded video. If the answer is "500+", route them directly to your calendar via Calendly.

## Phase 1: The Discovery Call

The biggest mistake technical founders make is launching into a live software demo in the first 5 minutes. The enterprise buyer does not care about your UI or your LLM. They care about their pain.

The first call is pure **Discovery**. Ask probing questions: *"How much time does your team currently spend manually classifying these PDFs? What is the error rate? How much does that cost you annually?"* You are gathering the ammunition required to build an undeniable ROI (Return on Investment) case. If you can prove your AI saves them $200,000 in labor, asking for $50,000 in software fees is a no-brainer.

## Phase 2: The Tailored Demo

Armed with their specific pain points, you schedule Call #2: The Demo. Do not show them generic features. If they told you they struggle with processing German legal contracts, your demo should exclusively show your AI processing German legal contracts.

Crucially, you must proactively address the CISO's (Chief Information Security Officer) fears during this demo. Explicitly state: *"Our architecture uses zero-retention APIs. Your proprietary contracts are processed in memory and immediately deleted. They are never used to train public models."* Winning the trust of the IT department is harder than winning the trust of the end-user.

## Phase 3: The Paid Proof of Concept (POC)

Massive organizations move slowly. Asking a VP to sign a 12-month, $100,000 contract for an unproven AI tool is incredibly risky for their career. You must de-risk the decision.

Offer a **Paid Proof of Concept (POC)**. *"Let's deploy the AI to just a 5-person pilot team for 30 days. It will cost $5,000. We will set hard metrics: if the AI successfully automates 80% of their workload, we move to the full $100k rollout."*

The POC must be paid (never free) to ensure the client assigns real internal resources to test it. If the POC succeeds, the final contract signature is practically guaranteed because the ROI is no longer theoretical; it is proven fact.

## Key Takeaways

- Enterprise AI contracts cannot be sold via a self-serve checkout page; you must gate high-tier plans behind a 'Book a Demo' funnel to initiate complex sales.

- Never demo the software immediately. Use the first 'Discovery Call' to extract the client's specific operational pain points and calculate their current financial losses.

- Tailor your software demo exclusively to the pain points discovered, and proactively address enterprise security fears (like data leakage and training model concerns).

- De-risk massive contracts by offering a Paid Proof of Concept (POC)—a small, 30-day pilot program to prove the AI's ROI in reality before the main contract is signed.

- Enterprise sales require convincing three distinct personas: the Champion (who wants the tool), the Economic Buyer (who signs the check), and the CISO (who ensures the data is secure).

## FAQ

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why can't I just put a 'Buy Now' button on my pricing page?

For a $50,000/year enterprise deployment, no one swipes a corporate card without approval. You must gate the tier behind a 'Book a Demo' button to initiate a complex sales process involving legal and IT.

### What is the purpose of the 'Discovery Call'?

It is not a product demo. It is an interrogation to find the client's deepest pain points. You gather the financial data required to prove the ROI of your software before you ever show it to them.

### How do I handle the 'Security Review' phase?

IT will block the sale if they believe your AI leaks data. Preemptively provide a 'Trust Center' on your site containing your SOC2 report and proof that you use zero-retention enterprise APIs.

### What is a 'Proof of Concept' (POC)?

Instead of asking a corporation to commit $100k immediately, you sell them a $5,000, 30-day trial limited to one team. You prove the AI's ROI in reality, making the $100k upsell significantly easier.
