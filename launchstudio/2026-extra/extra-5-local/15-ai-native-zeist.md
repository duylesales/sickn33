"AI native" has become one of those terms that gets used constantly and defined rarely. If you're a founder in Zeist who built your first product with Lovable or Bolt and someone called you AI native, it's worth pausing on what that actually means — because the definition determines what you still need to do before launch, not just how you got started.

## Defining AI Native Without the Buzzword

An AI-native founder is someone who builds software primarily by directing an AI tool — describing what they want in natural language and letting the tool generate the interface, logic, and often the initial backend — rather than writing code line by line themselves. It's a genuinely new way to start a company. Ten years ago, a non-technical founder in Zeist with a great idea for a wellness or health product needed a co-founder, a freelance developer, or tens of thousands of euros before anything existed. Now they can have a working prototype in a weekend.

But "AI native" describes how you started, not how ready you are to launch. This is where the term gets misused: founders assume that because their entire product emerged from an AI conversation, the AI conversation also handled everything a traditional development process would have covered — security review, data architecture, compliance considerations, payment infrastructure. It didn't. AI tools are exceptional at generating a working, good-looking prototype. They are not built to ask "what happens when this handles real customer data" or "is this GDPR-compliant" or "will this survive 500 concurrent users." Those questions require a different kind of expertise layered on top.

## Why This Distinction Matters Especially in Zeist

Zeist, in the province of Utrecht, has a distinct character among the founders we work with — it's home to a concentration of wellness, sustainability, and green-economy businesses, partly a legacy of the conference and retreat culture the town is known for, alongside a mix of corporate offices tucked into its wooded surroundings. Founders building in this space are often creating products that handle sensitive personal data: health metrics, wellness tracking, behavioral information people share more freely than they realize.

Being AI native and building in a data-sensitive category is a combination that raises the stakes on what "production ready" actually requires. A wellness app that mishandles user data isn't just a bug — in a market where trust is the entire product, it's existential. Roughly 80% of AI-built projects never reach production, and for founders in categories like wellness, health, or sustainability, the ones that do reach production but skip a proper security and compliance review often don't stay there long once users or regulators start paying attention.

## Real example

### An AI-Native Wellness Founder in Zeist Learns Where the Label Stops Covering Her

Femke van Beek, based in Zeist, built MindGarden — a guided meditation and mood-tracking app aimed at corporate wellness programs — using Lovable. She considered herself fully AI native: no code written by hand, no developer hired, the entire product directed through prompts over three weeks. MindGarden looked polished and had already been demoed to two Zeist-based wellness consultancies interested in white-labeling it for their corporate clients.

One consultancy's IT contact asked a direct question during due diligence: how was mood-tracking data stored, and was it encrypted at rest? Femke didn't know. She brought MindGarden to LaunchStudio for review. We found that sensitive mood and journal entries were stored as plain text with no encryption, and — separately — the app's authentication tokens didn't expire, meaning a stolen session could be reused indefinitely. We implemented encryption at rest for sensitive fields, added proper token expiration and refresh logic, and documented the data handling approach so Femke could answer compliance questions confidently going forward.

**Result:** MindGarden passed both consultancies' due diligence reviews and signed its first white-label contract within three weeks.

> *"Being AI native got me a real product fast. It didn't teach me what 'encrypted at rest' meant, or that I needed it. That gap could have cost me both deals."*
> — **Femke van Beek, Founder, MindGarden (Zeist)**

**Cost & Timeline:** €1,300 (data encryption, auth token rework, compliance documentation) — completed in 7 business days.
