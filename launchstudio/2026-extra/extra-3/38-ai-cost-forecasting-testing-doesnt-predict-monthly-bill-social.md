Real customers started submitting entire landing pages and email sequences, sometimes thousands of words, at the exact same price. Her margin was in trouble before she even noticed. 📉

The AI cost you watch during development and what a genuinely active product costs each month are two different numbers — and the gap isn't a mistake, it's structural. 🧠

❌ Development testing happens in short, deliberate bursts — nothing about it resembles how a real customer uses the product across a full session
❌ Per-request cost scales with input length in ways short test inputs never exercise, meaning founders systematically underestimate real per-request cost
❌ Usage frequency assumptions from solo testing have zero bearing on how often real customers will actually use a feature at scale
❌ A pricing model built on an inaccurate cost assumption can be unprofitable per customer — discovered only after customers are already paying the wrong price

✅ Build a deliberately realistic usage scenario — not your own testing pattern, but a plausible full month of real customer behavior — before committing to pricing
✅ Calculate cost against that realistic scenario, including honest estimates for input length and frequency
✅ If a gap surfaces post-launch, restructure through tiered pricing or grandfather existing customers rather than an abrupt, trust-damaging hike
