🚨 A prospective enterprise customer asked Esmee to explain exactly how KlantAssist "learns your brand voice automatically" — a line straight from her own marketing copy. Mid-conversation, she realized she couldn't describe a mechanism that didn't actually exist. 😳

"AI-powered" gets compressed into whatever phrase sounds most impressive — and nobody checks if the compressed version is still true. 🧠

❌ A basic rule-based system gets called "AI-powered" because it touches an AI API somewhere, overstating how much value actually comes from it
❌ "Learns from your data" and "gets smarter over time" are checkable claims — many products run a static, unchanging model behind that language
❌ Marketing copy implying full automation, when a human review step is still actually required, sets an expectation the product doesn't deliver
❌ Regulatory attention on AI marketing claims is rising — several jurisdictions now treat overstatement as a consumer-protection issue, not just aggressive copy

✅ For every AI claim in your copy, ask if you could concretely explain the mechanism that makes it true
✅ Describe the outcome for the user rather than the underlying mechanism — easier to keep accurate as the product evolves
✅ Correct the copy to match current capability, then separately decide whether to build toward what was implied
