🚨 Gerben tested GewasLog from his kitchen table the entire time he built it — stable wifi, quiet room, every feature working perfectly. Then real farmers took it into actual fields with no signal, and the app's core logging feature simply stopped working. They ended up writing notes on paper and re-entering them later, defeating the whole point. 🌾

"It works" meant "it works from my office." For agricultural users, that's not the environment the product actually lives in. 🧠

❌ The app assumed a live connection for every interaction, including the core logging feature itself
❌ The AI-generated risk assessment had no offline fallback — the entire core value proposition broke with no signal
❌ Testing exclusively from a well-connected home office never once surfaced the real-world condition
❌ Sync conflicts loom when offline sessions eventually reconnect, if the architecture wasn't built to merge them

✅ Rebuild the core flow around local-first data storage — full offline recording, automatic sync once back in range
✅ Add a cached, periodically-updated model that works with reasonable accuracy without a live AI connection
✅ Decide what must work offline from early architecture — retrofitting later is considerably more disruptive
