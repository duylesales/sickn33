Every provider in this space — LaunchStudio included — makes claims about readiness, security, and reliability, and this content series has spent considerable effort teaching founders to hold those exact kinds of claims to a specific, checkable standard rather than accepting confident language at face value. It would be inconsistent to make that argument throughout dozens of articles and then quietly exempt our own claims from the same scrutiny, so this final piece applies the standard directly to us.

## The One Question, Restated Plainly

Across this entire content series, the recurring diagnostic has been the same: is a given claim specific and verifiable, or is it vague and reassuring. "It's secure" is not a checkable claim. "Authentication is enforced server-side, verified by testing an unauthorized request directly against the API and confirming a 403 response" is a checkable claim. The single question worth asking of any provider, including LaunchStudio, is simply: can you make your claim specific enough that I could verify it myself, or with a second, independent technical opinion, if I wanted to.

## Applying This to LaunchStudio's Own Claims Directly

When LaunchStudio says a codebase has been hardened, that claim should come with the same specificity this series has argued for throughout — which secrets were found and rotated, which specific authorization pattern was implemented and how it was tested, which dependencies were reviewed and why. A founder is entitled to ask for exactly this level of detail from us, the same way this series has repeatedly encouraged founders to ask it of any freelancer, agency, or vendor.

## Why This Standard Should Apply Without Exception

The moment any provider — including one that's spent an entire content series arguing for specificity over reassurance — asks to be trusted based on confidence and reputation alone rather than checkable detail, that's precisely the pattern this series has warned against throughout, regardless of who's making the claim. Consistency requires that the standard apply universally, not selectively to competitors while exempting ourselves.

## What This Means Practically for a Founder Evaluating LaunchStudio Specifically

Ask for the specific artifacts covered elsewhere in this series' guidance on evaluating an audit's rigor — a defined scope, specific findings, described verification methods, clear remediation summaries. Ask Manifera's team to explain, in plain language, exactly what was tested and how, the same diagnostic questions this series has equipped non-technical founders to ask of anyone. A confident answer isn't sufficient on its own; a specific one is what actually matters, from us as much as from anyone else.
