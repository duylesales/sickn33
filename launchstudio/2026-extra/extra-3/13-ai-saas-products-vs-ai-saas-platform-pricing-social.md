🚨 Daniël called WerkflowMotor a "platform" in his pitch deck because it sounded more ambitious — while pricing it with a single flat monthly fee the entire time. Then a few customers started building genuinely platform-scale usage on top of it. His pricing never caught up. 📉

A product and a platform aren't just different words — they're different pricing questions entirely. 🧠

❌ Platform language shows up in a pitch deck before the architecture — real extensibility, stable APIs, flexible pricing — is actually built to support it
❌ A flat, per-seat SaaS price works for predictable usage per customer; it breaks down when one customer's usage is 100x another's
❌ Platforms serving other developers' systems need higher-stakes auth, rate limiting, and versioning than a single-purpose product ever does
❌ Flat pricing quietly creates a margin problem as your heaviest users scale past what the price was ever set for

✅ Honestly identify whether you're building a contained product or a genuine platform before you price it
✅ Restructure pricing around actual usage volume for platform-scale segments, keeping flat pricing where usage stays predictable
✅ Review API stability and versioning to confirm your architecture can actually support the usage it's now being asked to carry
