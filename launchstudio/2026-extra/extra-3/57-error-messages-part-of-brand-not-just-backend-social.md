🚨 Bo poured real care into KlantContact's tone everywhere — onboarding, everyday interface, all warm and carefully written. Except error messages, which still showed Lovable's raw default: "Error: Request failed with status code 500." One payment hiccup, and a customer briefly wondered if the whole product was as polished as it seemed. 💬

The moment something breaks carries more weight than any moment things go smoothly — and it usually gets the least design attention. 🧠

❌ Generic, technical error text signals — intentionally or not — that the product wasn't built with the customer's actual failure experience in mind
❌ Structured error handling covers technical correctness, not what the resulting message actually says to the person seeing it
❌ AI coding tools generate technically correct error handling by default, with zero reason to also write brand-appropriate customer language unless specifically prompted
❌ A customer's emotional state at the exact moment of failure is already elevated — frustrated, confused — so whatever they see there lands harder than equivalent polish elsewhere

✅ Write error messages in your product's actual voice and tone — the same care applied to marketing copy and onboarding
✅ Be specific about what happened and what the customer can actually do, never expose raw stack traces or codes with no context
✅ Deliberately trigger every failure condition and review what customers actually see, rather than discovering gaps one complaint at a time
