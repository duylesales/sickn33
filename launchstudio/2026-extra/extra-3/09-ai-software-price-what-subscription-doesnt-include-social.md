The subscription price buys generation capability. It was never designed to represent the total cost of a launchable product — and nobody tells a first-time founder that up front. 🧠

❌ A monthly AI tool subscription doesn't cover hosting, a production-grade database, payment processing fees, or the production-hardening work itself
❌ Infrastructure costs scale with actual usage, meaning early testing-stage bills are a poor predictor of what a genuinely active product costs to run
❌ Ongoing costs — monitoring, backups, security updates — don't stop the moment a product launches; they're a recurring line nobody budgeted for
❌ A first-time founder has no prior reference point for what a "complete" cost structure looks like, so the subscription price becomes the plan by default

✅ Get the complete, realistic cost picture — subscription, one-time hardening, ongoing operational expense — before committing to a launch date
✅ Budget a reasonable initial range based on comparable products, with an explicit plan to revisit once real usage data exists
✅ Finding the full number before a public launch date turns a hard surprise into a planning decision instead of a broken promise
