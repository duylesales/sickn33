🚨 Fenna brought on a part-time contractor to help with customer support at SollicitatieScan during a busy hiring season — sharing the single admin login the team had always used. When the contractor's engagement ended months later, Fenna realized the credential had never been rotated. The former contractor technically still had full access to every applicant's raw screening data. 😳

A shared login that feels completely harmless with one founder becomes a real liability the moment any team, however small, exists around it. 🧠

❌ A solo founder's full access isn't a decision — there's simply no internal access question to ask with one person in the building
❌ Nothing about adding a second team member naturally prompts a founder to revisit access as a formal question
❌ AI product prompts and outputs often contain far more candid, sensitive detail than a typical database record — raising the stakes of internal access specifically
❌ A shared credential logs no per-person activity, so there's no way to confirm — or rule out — what a former contractor actually saw

✅ Move to individual accounts so access can be granted, revoked, and audited per person, not shared as one all-or-nothing login
✅ Scope access by role — a support contractor doesn't need the same raw prompt visibility an engineer genuinely does
✅ Add a basic audit log recording who accessed what and when, so there's a real answer ready if it's ever asked

At **LaunchStudio**, internal access control is a standard checkpoint the moment a founding team adds its second or third contributor — mirroring the same access-scoping discipline Manifera applies internally across its own global teams. 🛡️

Her result: individual, role-scoped accounts, full credential rotation, and an ongoing process for granting and revoking access — closing a gap that had existed invisibly since her very first hire. 🚀
