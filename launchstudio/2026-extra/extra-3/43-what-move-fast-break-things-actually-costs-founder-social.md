🚨 Robin built FlexPlan's entire team culture around "ship fast, don't overthink it" — a phrase borrowed wholesale from startup lore. When he later needed to introduce real review discipline, his own team pushed back, because they'd been hired and praised specifically for embodying the old motto. 😅

🧠 "Move fast and break things" made sense for a company with vast scale and a huge team ready to fix breakage instantly. It means something very different for five people where one broken feature is a big chunk of the whole product.

❌ An inherited philosophy applied uncritically becomes a cultural default, not a deliberate choice matched to actual stakes
❌ It signals that carefulness and verification are lower-status than raw shipping speed — exactly the wrong signal for AI-native building
❌ It attracts and rewards generation speed over independent verification judgment
❌ Introducing discipline later feels, to an already-established team, like betraying the culture that got them there

✅ Reframe around reversible-versus-irreversible decisions, not "fast" versus "careful"
✅ Keep genuine speed on genuinely reversible calls — don't swing to blanket caution
✅ Introduce deliberate rigor specifically for the higher-stakes categories, framed as evolution, not contradiction
