A vendor's pitch deck is subject to the exact same gap between marketing language and real mechanism that founders are told to check in their own product. 🧠

❌ "AI-based security" covers both genuinely valuable anomaly detection and a basic rule engine with AI-branded language slapped on top
❌ Vendors pressed for concrete detail sometimes can't describe what their AI component actually detects beyond generic phrasing
❌ Detection turns out, on closer inspection, to be a standard rule-based system — nothing meaningfully different from a traditional approach
❌ That "AI premium" gets charged anyway, at a real cost markup over comparable, honestly-labeled alternatives

✅ Ask specifically: what does the AI detect that a rule-based approach wouldn't, how was it trained, what's the actual false-positive rate
✅ Request a technical reference or case study — reluctance to provide one is informative on its own
✅ Don't pay more for AI branding alone; cost should track actual capability and fit for your specific risk profile
