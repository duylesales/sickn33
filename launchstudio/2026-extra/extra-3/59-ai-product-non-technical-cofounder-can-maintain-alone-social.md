Most founding teams never stress-test what happens if the technical founder disappears — until they actually have to. 🧠

❌ Critical infrastructure access tied to the technical founder's personal login, not a shared, company-owned account structure
❌ Documentation covering only what the codebase does — nothing on how to handle routine operational tasks or who to contact
❌ No support relationship the non-technical cofounder could reach independently of the technical founder's personal availability
❌ The assumption that the technical founder will always be reasonably available, never actually stress-tested until real circumstances force it

✅ Set up properly shared, company-owned credentials and access — not locked behind one person's personal accounts
✅ Write plain-language operational documentation: how to check if something's down, who to contact for what
✅ Establish an ongoing support relationship the non-technical cofounder can reach on their own
