"Ship fast" and "test thoroughly first" aren't actually in conflict — they apply to two different categories of risk. 🧠

❌ Applying "ship fast" uniformly to EVERYTHING, including security, is a common founder mistake
❌ A disliked feature is fully reversible — remove it, users move on, no lasting damage
❌ A security incident or duplicated payment is NOT reversible the same way — trust doesn't reset
❌ "I'll fix it if it becomes a problem" assumes you'll find out before something bad happens

✅ The real line: reversible vs. irreversible consequence, not "features vs. infrastructure"
✅ Ship features fast, iterate on real feedback — that instinct is genuinely correct for product risk
✅ Secrets, auth, data integrity, payment correctness need deliberate pre-launch verification
✅ Ask before shipping: can I just fix this, or has something already happened that can't be undone?
