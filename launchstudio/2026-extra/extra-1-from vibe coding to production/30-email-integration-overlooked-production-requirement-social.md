"It sent in testing" and "it reaches real inboxes" are two completely different claims. 🧠

❌ Your own test inbox is lenient — real mail providers filter live app traffic much harder
❌ No SPF/DKIM/DMARC = mail servers can't verify your email is actually legitimate
❌ A password reset landing in spam = a user locked out with zero path to recover
❌ This churn is invisible — it never generates a support ticket telling you why

✅ SPF tells servers who's authorized to send on your domain's behalf
✅ DKIM cryptographically proves the email actually came from you, unaltered
✅ DMARC tells servers what to do when those checks fail — and gets weighted by spam filters
✅ A dedicated sending subdomain protects your domain's reputation long-term
