🚨 Her first audit report landed with a mix of critical, medium, and low findings — genuinely overwhelming for a non-technical founder. With the right framework, it became a decision she could actually make instead of just hoping it was fine. 📋

A finding isn't an accusation your build was done poorly. It's the expected output of exactly the adversarial testing every AI-generated codebase warrants. 🧠

❌ Critical and high findings = exploitable immediately, at scale, minimal effort — resolve these before launch
❌ A well-written finding needs all four parts: what was tested, what was found, why it matters, how it's fixed
❌ Missing "what was tested" or "why it matters"? That's worth asking about directly
❌ A report with zero findings on a first-time review deserves MORE scrutiny, not less

✅ Ask: "Can you explain this in plain language?" — a good auditor should translate any finding
✅ Ask: "What's the realistic worst case if this isn't fixed?" to calibrate real urgency
✅ A resolved finding should say specifically what changed — not "fixed auth," the actual before-and-after
✅ Launch confidently once all critical/high items are resolved and you understand what's deliberately deferred
