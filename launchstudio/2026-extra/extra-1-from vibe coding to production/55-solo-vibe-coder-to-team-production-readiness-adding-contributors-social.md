The moment a second person touches your codebase, several things that were tolerable solo become genuinely necessary. 🧠

❌ A solo founder has full context on every change by definition — a team doesn't, structurally
❌ "Code review" is meaningless for one person — the instant a team exists, it becomes your main defense against the self-review blind spot
❌ Everyone sharing full access by default because it was simplest with one founder doesn't scale to a team
❌ Every new contributor is a fresh opportunity to silently reintroduce a gap that was already closed

✅ A real CI pipeline, enforced without exception, catches one contributor's change breaking what another built
✅ Explicit, not implicit, access control — exactly what each contributor needs, nothing more
✅ Enough documentation that onboarding doesn't depend entirely on synchronous, ad hoc explanation
✅ Treat adding any second contributor as the trigger to formalize what worked informally before
