⚙️ Job Willems built TaakSlim, a task automation app, with Bolt — an "AI assist" feature that suggested task groupings on top of an otherwise manual workflow. But his pitch deck and roadmap described it as fully "AI native" from top to bottom.

Calling AI-assist a product "AI native" (or the reverse) isn't a labeling mistake — it's a wrong bet on where your infrastructure money should go. 🧠

❌ Infrastructure was sized for constant model calls that were never actually the bottleneck
❌ The real manual-workflow backend, handling the bulk of real usage, was left under-resourced
❌ When a real customer arrived with real volume, task creation slowed and team sync started lagging
❌ Nobody had planned for the parts of the app that were actually straining under load

✅ Get an honest architecture review to confirm which category the product actually falls into
✅ Re-prioritize engineering around the real bottleneck — in this case, database indexing and query optimization
✅ Leave the parts that already work (the AI suggestion feature) alone instead of over-investing there
