"Delete my account" turns out to mean something much more involved than removing one row from one table. Few AI-built prototypes were ever specifically asked to handle that. 🧠

❌ A "delete account" feature removing just the login record can genuinely feel complete during testing — account gone, login fails, demo looks done
❌ What typically doesn't get addressed: data scattered across other tables — booking history, messages, uploaded documents, activity logs
❌ GDPR's right to erasure requires personal data actually removed across a system, not just login access revoked
❌ This gap creates real time pressure the moment a genuine request arrives — GDPR specifies response timeframes, forcing a scramble

✅ Map every location a user's personal data actually lives across the application
✅ Build a genuine deletion or anonymization process addressing all of them
✅ Test against a real account rather than assuming it works from the primary account table alone
