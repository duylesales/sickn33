A checkbox at signup proves consent to whatever terms existed *that day*. It says nothing about the policy you're running today. 🧠

❌ Consent was stored as a single unversioned flag with no link to specific policy text
❌ When the practice updated its data-sharing policy, hundreds of existing patients were never re-prompted to review or accept it
❌ They kept booking sessions under a consent record tied to a policy that no longer reflected how their data was actually used
❌ Nothing in a prompt like "add a consent form to onboarding" implies re-consent when terms change — so it never gets built

✅ Treat the consent form itself as a versioned entity — every published version gets a unique ID and timestamp
✅ Link every patient's consent record to the specific version they agreed to
✅ Trigger an automatic re-consent gate the moment a new version publishes, blocking further bookings until confirmed
