A SaaS founder scaling past their home market usually discovers their VAT bug the same way: an accountant flags it during a quarterly review, months after the invoices went out. By then there isn't one bad invoice to fix — there are hundreds, each one technically non-compliant, and each one a potential liability with a different EU tax authority. This is one of the quietest, most expensive gaps in AI-generated billing systems, and it rarely gets caught in a demo.

## Why "it calculates tax" isn't the same as "it calculates tax correctly"

Ask Lovable, Bolt, or Cursor to "add VAT to checkout" and you'll get working code — a percentage applied to a subtotal, a line item on the invoice, a total that adds up. It looks correct because arithmetically it is. What it almost never does is apply the *right* rate for the *right* reason, because EU VAT for digital services doesn't work off a single number. Under the EU's VAT rules for digital services, B2C sales are taxed at the buyer's country rate, not the seller's — meaning a SaaS company based in the Netherlands selling to a customer in France charges French VAT, not Dutch VAT. B2B sales, by contrast, typically use the reverse-charge mechanism if the buyer provides a valid VAT number, shifting the tax liability to the buyer entirely.

An AI-generated checkout flow has no reason to know any of this unless someone specifies it in exhaustive detail, and most founders don't know to ask for it until it's already wrong in production. The typical failure mode looks exactly like the seed case below: the seller's home-country rate gets hardcoded and applied to every invoice, regardless of where the buyer actually is. It passes every test because the founder is the one testing it, from the founder's own country.

## What correct multi-currency, multi-VAT billing actually requires

Getting this right means the billing system needs to determine the customer's location using at least two independent signals (IP address and billing address, per EU evidence rules), store and validate VAT numbers via the VIES system for B2B reverse charge, apply the correct rate per country automatically as it updates, and generate invoices that itemize the applicable rate and legal basis for every line. None of that is generated by a prompt like "add Stripe checkout" — it requires someone who has built cross-border billing before to specify it explicitly.

### An AI-Native Founder in Action: The Invoicing Tool That Got Its Own Tax Wrong

Quinten Adriaans, a founder based in Roosendaal, built FactuurFlow — an invoicing SaaS aimed at freelancers — using Lovable. The irony wasn't lost on him: a tool built to help freelancers invoice correctly was itself invoicing its own subscribers incorrectly. Every customer, regardless of which EU country they signed up from, was charged VAT at the Dutch rate, because that's the only rate the AI-generated billing logic had ever been given.

The problem stayed invisible for months because FactuurFlow's early customers were mostly Dutch. It surfaced the moment freelancers in Belgium and Germany started signing up and their invoices didn't match what their own accountants expected. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does my AI-built SaaS charge the wrong VAT rate for foreign customers?

Because the AI tool defaults to a single hardcoded rate — usually your own country's — unless you explicitly specify buyer-location-based tax logic, which most prompts never include.

### Is this only a problem for larger SaaS companies?

No. Any SaaS company selling digital services to EU consumers is legally required to charge the buyer's country rate from the very first cross-border sale, regardless of company size.

### How does Manifera's team approach VAT logic differently than a typical freelancer?

Manifera's engineers have built billing and compliance systems for enterprise clients including Vodafone, so they treat VAT determination as a structured requirement — location detection, rate lookup, reverse-charge handling — rather than a single tax percentage bolted onto checkout.

### What's the risk of leaving this unfixed?

Incorrect invoices create real liability with multiple tax authorities simultaneously, and the longer it runs, the more historical invoices eventually need correction and reissue.

### Does LaunchStudio only fix this for EU-based founders?

No — LaunchStudio's Singapore office supports founders selling into the EU from anywhere, since the buyer's location determines VAT obligations regardless of where the company itself is based.
