🚨 An agency preparing for an internal data protection review hit a question they couldn't answer: how long is candidate data actually kept? The honest answer was that full profiles of rejected candidates — including sensitive interview notes from years earlier — were still sitting in the live database with no policy governing any of it. 😳

Deletion isn't a feature you demo. It's an absence you only notice once a regulator, a candidate, or an audit asks the question directly. 🧠

❌ Cursor's candidate database defaulted to keeping everything indefinitely — rows don't expire on their own
❌ CVs, personal notes, and sensitive interview disclosures (health, salary history, reasons for leaving) sat unbounded, well past any defensible retention window
❌ There was no automated process, no audit log, and no documented policy the agency could show a candidate or auditor
❌ The gap is invisible in daily use — recruiters intake, track, and match candidates without ever touching a retention feature that doesn't exist

✅ A defined retention period per data category, set deliberately rather than left undefined
✅ An automated process that flags or deletes records once they pass that threshold — no relying on memory
✅ A clear audit log and a documented policy the agency can show candidates and clients alike
