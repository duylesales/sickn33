🚨 A resident in Floor Achterberg's neighborhood app posted an inflammatory complaint naming a specific neighbor — and her AI "highlight" feature read it, judged it high-engagement, and pinned it to the top of every resident's feed. Nobody approved that. No moderator saw it. 😳

🧠 Once an AI feature reads, summarizes, or ranks user content, you have a second publisher in your app — and it needs its own review logic, not a pass on the original filter.

❌ The AI summarizer had no sense that a post might be unsuitable to feature — it just compressed what it was given
❌ Content that light moderation would've caught on the way in got laundered through the AI layer and came out looking "official"
❌ Neither raw posts nor AI-generated summaries were ever checked against any moderation policy — the feature was built for engagement, not safety
❌ A pinned "community highlight" carries implicit endorsement users don't get with a random post

✅ Run raw posts through a policy classifier before they're even eligible for AI summarization
✅ Never let an AI feature auto-promote content to a featured slot without one manual approval
✅ Log what the AI selected and why, so patterns are auditable later
