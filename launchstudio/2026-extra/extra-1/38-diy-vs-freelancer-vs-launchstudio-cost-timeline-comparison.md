Every founder facing the vibe-coding-to-production gap chooses among the same three realistic paths: do it yourself, if you have or can develop the technical skill; hire a freelancer or small agency; or work with a specialist provider like LaunchStudio. Each path has a real, honest cost-and-risk profile, and the right choice depends on your specific technical background, timeline, and risk tolerance more than on any single path being universally superior.

## The DIY Path

**Cost:** Primarily your own time, which is real but often undercounted — the weekend framework covered elsewhere in this series describes roughly 8-12 hours for a technically fluent founder to address the core gaps, though this assumes existing comfort with the specific tools and concepts involved, which not every technical founder has by default.

**Timeline:** Fastest in principle, since there's no coordination overhead with an external party, though realistically extended by learning curve for any unfamiliar concept — a founder learning proper authorization patterns for the first time takes considerably longer than the baseline estimate assumes.

**Where the real risk concentrates:** Self-review blind spots, covered specifically in this series' guidance on Cursor users — reviewing your own implementation tends to confirm it does what you intended, not surface what you didn't intend, meaning even a technically skilled founder doing this path alone risks missing exactly the adversarial gaps a second party would be more likely to catch.

## The Freelancer/Agency Path

**Cost:** Typically €5,000-€20,000 for freelancer or small agency scopes, reflecting both the actual work and, often, a less efficient per-hour rate than a specialist team working across many similar engagements, plus a real risk of scope creep if the initial assessment underestimates what's actually needed.

**Timeline:** Highly variable, ranging from a few weeks to several months, depending heavily on the specific freelancer's availability, familiarity with AI-generated codebase patterns specifically, and whether the engagement is their primary focus or one of several concurrent projects.

**Where the real risk concentrates:** The freelancer-competence gap covered elsewhere in this series — a freelancer without specific experience in AI-generated codebase patterns may not know to check for the exact recurring gaps this series describes, potentially producing a "completed" engagement that still leaves core vulnerabilities unaddressed, discovered only later.

## The LaunchStudio Path

**Cost:** €800-€7,500 depending on scope, reflecting Manifera's efficiency across many similar engagements and specific, dedicated familiarity with AI-generated codebase patterns rather than general-purpose development pricing.

**Timeline:** One to three weeks at a fixed, pre-agreed timeline, reflecting a process specifically built around this exact category of work rather than general custom development estimation.

**Where the real risk concentrates:** Primarily around fit and communication — ensuring the scoping conversation accurately captures what your specific product needs, and that expectations about what's included versus what requires a separate conversation are clearly established upfront, a risk addressed through the audit-first, transparent-artifact approach covered throughout this series.

## Why Direct Cost Comparison Alone Is Misleading

Comparing only the headline cost of each path misses that the paths aren't interchangeable in what they actually deliver — DIY delivers a result entirely bounded by your own current skill and available time; a freelancer delivers a result bounded by that specific individual's experience with this exact codebase category; a specialist delivers a result informed by pattern recognition across many similar engagements. The "cheapest" option on paper isn't cheapest if it requires redoing the work later after a freelancer's gap in AI-code-specific experience surfaces, or if your own DIY blind spot goes undiscovered until a genuine incident forces the question.

## A Practical Way to Choose

If you have genuine, demonstrated technical fluency and available time, and are comfortable accepting the self-review blind spot risk (perhaps mitigated by having a second technical person review your critical security decisions specifically), DIY is a reasonable path. If you have an existing trusted freelancer relationship and can verify their specific AI-codebase experience using the diagnostic questions and audit artifacts covered throughout this series, that path can work well. If neither applies, a specialist provider with demonstrated, specific experience in this exact category typically offers the best combination of cost, timeline certainty, and risk mitigation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is DIY ever genuinely the wrong choice, even for a highly technical founder?

It can be, specifically if the self-review blind spot risk isn't mitigated somehow — a highly technical founder working entirely alone, with no second reviewer for the adversarial and security-specific testing covered throughout this series, carries real risk regardless of general coding skill.

### How can I evaluate a freelancer's actual AI-codebase-specific experience before committing, similar to what Floor did?

Asking directly about their process for the specific patterns covered throughout this series — how they check for hardcoded secrets across git history, how they verify authentication at the API level — and evaluating whether their answer is specific or vague is the direct method, mirroring the broader diagnostic approach covered elsewhere in this series.

### Does LaunchStudio's fixed pricing model ever change once an engagement begins, or is the initial quote final?

The scoping conversation is specifically designed to produce an accurate quote before commitment, based on an actual look at your codebase rather than a guess — significant scope changes discovered mid-engagement would be discussed transparently rather than silently absorbed into cost, similar to the transparency principle covered in this series' audit-artifact guidance.

### Is it possible to combine paths, like using a freelancer for design work and a specialist for production hardening, similar to the white-label guidance covered elsewhere?

Yes — this is a common and reasonable combination, particularly relevant for founders who have a trusted freelancer relationship for frontend or design work specifically, while sourcing the backend production-readiness work from a specialist with dedicated depth in that specific area.

### How much does the specific AI coding tool used (Lovable, Bolt, Cursor, v0) affect which path makes the most sense?

It affects the specific scope more than the choice of path itself — a v0-based prototype needing a full backend built, as covered elsewhere in this series, is a larger scope regardless of which path delivers it, though the underlying DIY-vs-freelancer-vs-specialist decision framework applies similarly across all four tools.
