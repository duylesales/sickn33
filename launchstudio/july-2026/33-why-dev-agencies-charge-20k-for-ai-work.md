# Why Dev Agencies Charge €20k for What AI Does in a Weekend

## Nội dung
If you pitched a SaaS idea to a development agency in 2023, you likely received a quote between €20,000 and €50,000, with a timeline of 3 to 6 months. Today, you can sign up for Lovable for $20 and build a functional prototype of that exact same idea over a weekend. Are agencies running a scam, or is the AI missing something critical? The answer lies in the shifting economics of software boilerplate.

            ## The Cost of Human Boilerplate

            Agencies do not charge €20k because they are greedy; they charge it because human labor is expensive. A traditional agency quote includes:

                - **Discovery & Wireframing (1 Week)**: A designer maps out every screen.

                - **Environment Setup (3 Days)**: Developers manually configure React, Next.js, and Supabase.

                - **Boilerplate Coding (2 Weeks)**: Developers manually type out standard components: login screens, navigation bars, profile settings, and data tables.

                - **Core Logic (2 Weeks)**: Building the actual unique feature of your app.

                - **Project Management**: Managing communication between you, the designer, and the devs.

            **The Reality**: 80% of what an agency builds for a new SaaS MVP is identical to every other SaaS MVP. It is boilerplate. And AI is the ultimate boilerplate destroyer.

            ## The AI Advantage: Infinite Velocity

            When you prompt an AI builder, it does not type code line by line. It instantly generates the boilerplate because it has seen the pattern for a "React Login Screen" ten million times. It skips the wireframing phase entirely by generating functional UI instantly.

            What took an agency 4 weeks of human labor to set up, the AI accomplishes in 40 seconds. The €20,000 quote isn't a scam; it's just the price of doing things the old, slow way.

            ## The 80/20 Trap: Where AI Fails

            If AI is so fast, why isn't everyone a millionaire SaaS founder? Because of the 80/20 rule. The AI gets you 80% of the way there almost instantly. It gives you the UI, the core logic, and the "wow" factor.

            But the final 20% is where prototypes die. AI builders prioritize visual functionality over production security. They use test-mode Stripe keys. They leave Supabase Row Level Security disabled. They hardcode sensitive API keys into frontend files. They do not set up error tracking.

            If you launch the AI's output without fixing that final 20%, you will experience data breaches, broken checkouts, and ruined reputations.

            ## The New Economic Model: The Hybrid Approach

            The smartest founders in 2026 are using a hybrid economic model to launch.

                1. **Phase 1 (The 80%)**: The founder acts as the product manager, using AI (Lovable, Cursor) to build the UI and core logic for $20.

                2. **Phase 2 (The 20%)**: The founder hires specialized engineers (like LaunchStudio) not to build the app from scratch, but to secure, harden, and deploy the AI's output.

            Because the specialized engineers aren't wasting hours typing out boilerplate UI, they can secure the database, implement live Stripe webhooks, and set up CI/CD pipelines in a fraction of the time. This drops the cost of a production-ready launch from €20,000 down to roughly €2,500.

            ## The Death of the MVP Agency

            Traditional agencies will survive, but only by moving upmarket to handle complex enterprise systems. The business model of charging non-technical founders €20,000 to build a standard SaaS MVP is dead. The future belongs to founders who leverage AI for velocity, and professional services for security.

            ## Key Takeaways

                - Traditional agencies charge €20k because they rely on slow, expensive human labor to write standard boilerplate code.

                - AI instantly generates that 80% boilerplate for $20.

                - AI fails at the final 20%: production security, live payment infrastructure, and safe deployment.

                - The modern approach is hybrid: Founders use AI to build the prototype, then hire specialists to secure and launch it.

                - This hybrid model reduces the cost of launching a SaaS by up to 90%.
